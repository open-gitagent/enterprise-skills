## Role
Guide enterprise account planning from intelligence gathering through execution to drive expansion revenue and multi-threaded relationships.

## Decision Framework

| Tier | Focus | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1 — Account Selection | ARR, total addressable spend, strategic value, relationship depth, competitive vulnerability | High on 1–4 + low on 5 = prime candidate; high on 5 = displacement playbook |
| 2 — Stakeholder Power Mapping | Influence (formal + informal) × disposition (champion/supporter/neutral/skeptic/blocker) | Coalition-build across alliances, rivalries, reporting lines |
| 3 — White Space | Product × BU matrix; score by trigger event + existing relationship + differentiation strength | Prioritize cells with all three signals |
| 4 — Competitive Positioning | Incumbent installed base, renewal timelines, dissatisfaction points | Sequence plays over 12–24 months to erode competitor position |
| 5 — Value Hypothesis & Roadmap | Quantified outcome per stakeholder; quarterly milestones with named owners | Trigger-based actions on leadership change, earnings call, tech refresh |

## Account Plan Scoring Model

| Dimension | Weight | Criteria |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Stakeholder Coverage | 20 pts | 10+ contacts mapped; influence/disposition scored; gaps identified |
| White Space Completeness | 15 pts | Full product × BU matrix populated; attractiveness scored |
| Competitive Intelligence | 15 pts | All incumbents documented with renewal dates and positioning |
| Value Hypotheses | 15 pts | Quantified outcomes per opportunity; tied to customer KPIs |
| Execution Roadmap | 15 pts | Quarterly milestones with owners; trigger-based actions defined |
| Relationship Depth | 10 pts | ≥2 contacts at Level 3+; executive sponsor engaged |
| Plan Freshness | 10 pts | Updated within last 30 days; signals integrated |

Plans <60 = incomplete. Plans ≥80 = execution-ready.

## Leading Indicators

| Indicator | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Pipeline-to-plan ratio | ≥2× account revenue target |
| Multi-thread depth (avg engaged contacts) | 10+ |
| Stakeholder coverage ratio at Level 2+ | >60% |
| Plan freshness (days since update) | <30 |

## Process

1. **Account Intelligence Gathering** — Pull CRM, 10-K, earnings transcripts, LinkedIn, technographics. Flag leadership transitions within last 90 days.
2. **Stakeholder Inventory & Mapping** — Record title, function, tenure, influence (1–5), disposition, priorities, success metrics, communication style. Benchmark: 10+ engaged contacts across departments.
3. **Relationship Strength Assessment** — Score Level 0 (no contact) through Level 4 (strategic partner). Target Level 3+ with ≥2 in economic buyer chain and 1 in technical chain.
4. **White Space & Opportunity Matrix** — Grid: rows = product/solution categories; columns = BU/geo/function. Populate with installed/trialing/competitor-held/greenfield status.
5. **Account-Level SWOT** — Specific to your position in this account; not a generic company SWOT.
6. **Competitive Landscape Documentation** — Per competitor: products installed, estimated contract value, renewal dates, known champion, strengths/weaknesses.
7. **Goal Setting & Initiative Design** — SMART goals over 12 months: revenue (expansion ARR), relationship (new Level 2+ stakeholders), strategic (exec sponsor, advisory board).
8. **Mutual Action Plan (MAP) Construction** — For active opportunities: joint milestones, decision dates, resource commitments, success criteria. MAPs drive 26% higher win rates.
9. **Rhythm of Business Integration** — Monthly plan review (30 min), quarterly strategic review (60 min), semi-annual exec sponsor alignment.
10. **Signal Monitoring & Plan Refresh** — Monitor leadership changes, M&A, earnings, tech RFPs, hiring patterns. Update within 48 hours of material signal. Plans >30 days without updates lose predictive value.

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| White Space Analysis | Mapping of product/service portfolio vs. customer org units to identify unpenetrated segments scored by trigger events, relationships, and differentiation |
| Multi-Threading | Multiple active relationships across functions, levels, and locations; deals with multi-thread engagement show 37% higher close rates |
| Champion | Stakeholder with influence, economic buyer access, and active internal advocacy—not merely a supporter |
| Economic Buyer | Individual with final budget authority; may not be the most senior person involved |
| Buying Group | Cross-functional stakeholders influencing a purchase; Gartner: 6–10 individuals; Forrester: up to 13 |
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | % of recurring revenue retained including expansion minus churn; >120% indicates strong expansion |
| Mutual Action Plan (MAP) | Shared buyer-seller document with joint milestones, deliverables, and decision dates |
| MEDDPICC | Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Implicate Pain, Champion, Competition |
| Blue Sheet | Miller Heiman/Korn Ferry Strategic Selling worksheet for analyzing buying influences and red flags |
| Stakeholder Disposition | Champion/supporter/neutral/skeptic/blocker; shifts with org changes and competitive activity |
| Value Hypothesis | Specific, quantified business outcome tied to customer KPIs or strategic initiatives |
| Account Health Score | Composite of adoption, relationship depth, support trends, NPS/CSAT, commercial engagement |
| Trigger Event | Leadership transition, M&A, regulatory mandate, tech refresh, or budget cycle creating a buying window |
| Paper Process | Customer internal procurement workflow: legal review, security, vendor registration, contract chain |
| Relationship Scoring | 0–4 scale from no contact to strategic partner; tracks development over time |
| Land and Expand | Initial scoped sale (land) with pre-designed expansion across BUs, use cases, or users |
| Single-Threading | Relying on one contact; creates catastrophic relationship and intelligence risk |
| Installed Base Penetration | % of customer's total addressable need served by your solutions; key white space input |
| Executive Sponsor Alignment | Pairing your executives with customer executives for strategic dialogue above deal-level selling |
| Competitive Displacement Play | Planned sequence to replace an incumbent, timed to contract renewal window |
| Account Segmentation Tier | Strategic/Growth/Maintain/Harvest classification determining planning investment and resource allocation |
| Buying Job | Gartner framework: problem identification, solution exploration, requirements building, supplier selection, validation, consensus creation |
| Warm Pipeline | Identified opportunities not yet formally qualified; leading indicator of plan effectiveness |

## Checklist

- [ ] Stakeholder map includes ≥10 contacts with influence score, disposition, and function documented
- [ ] White space matrix complete — every product × BU cell populated with penetration status
- [ ] At least 3 quantified value hypotheses tied to customer strategic priorities or KPIs
- [ ] Competitive landscape documented for every incumbent with products, estimated contract value, and renewal dates
- [ ] Executive sponsor pairing established with ≥1 meeting in last 90 days
- [ ] Plan links directly to CRM pipeline — every planned opportunity has a corresponding pipeline record
- [ ] SWOT is account-specific, not a generic company SWOT
- [ ] Mutual Action Plans exist for all active opportunities
- [ ] Customer success data integrated — NPS/CSAT, adoption metrics, support trends referenced
- [ ] Plan updated within last 30 days with new signals incorporated
- [ ] Cross-functional input documented from CS, SE, professional services, and/or product
- [ ] Relationship gap analysis identifies functions or levels with zero contacts and proposes actions
- [ ] Revenue target supported by pipeline — active + warm pipeline covers ≥2× the 12-month target
- [ ] Buying group dynamics mapped — alliances, conflicts, and decision governance documented
- [ ] Quarterly milestones have named owners and defined deliverables
