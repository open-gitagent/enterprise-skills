## Role
Design and execute multi-phase onboarding programs transitioning new hires from offer acceptance to fully integrated, productive team members within 90 days.

## SHRM Four C's Maturity Assessment

| C | Focus | Score 1–5 | Priority |
|---|-------|-----------|---------|
| Compliance | Legal forms, policy acknowledgments, regulatory trainings completed on time and documented | Score below 3 = immediate remediation before expanding other phases |
| Clarification | New hire understands role, performance standards, decision authority, and success metrics within 30 days | |
| Culture | Mission, values, formal and informal norms, communication styles explicitly taught | |
| Connection | Structured introductions, buddy assignments, cross-functional networking, manager 1:1 cadence in place | |

Only ~20% of organizations reach SHRM Level 3 Proactive Onboarding. 80% cover Compliance + partial Clarification while neglecting Culture and Connection — the two elements most correlated with long-term retention.

## Phase-Gate Progression

| Gate | Entry Criteria |
|------|---------------|
| Pre-boarding → Orientation | All compliance paperwork submitted; equipment provisioned; welcome materials delivered; manager introduction complete |
| Orientation → Role Training | New hire articulates company mission; understands reporting structure; completed policy acknowledgments; met immediate team |
| Role Training → Integration | Baseline competency on core tools/systems; required training modules completed; regular 1:1 cadence established with manager |
| Integration → Steady State | 90-day performance milestones met; peer network established; autonomous task execution; feedback cycle completed |

## Federal Compliance Requirements

| Requirement | Authority | Deadline | Key Detail |
|-------------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Form I-9 | USCIS / 8 CFR §274a.2 | Section 1: on or before Day 1; Section 2: within 3 business days of hire | Cannot specify which documents to present; retain for 3 years after hire or 1 year after termination |
| E-Verify | 8 USC §1324a | Within 3 business days of hire (after I-9) | Mandatory for federal contractors (FAR 52.222-54) and certain states (AZ, MS, AL, SC, NC) |
| Form W-4 | IRS | Before first payroll | Retain for 4 years; if employee refuses, withhold at highest single-filer rate |
| State W-4 / Withholding | State tax authority | Before first payroll | Required in all states with income tax; 41 states + DC; 9 no-income-tax states: AK, FL, NV, NH, SD, TN, TX, WA, WY |
| New Hire Reporting | PRWORA / 42 USC §653a | Within 20 days of hire (some states: 10 or 15 days) | Report to state where employee works; includes name, address, SSN, hire date, company FEIN |
| OSHA Safety Training | 29 CFR 1910 (General); 29 CFR 1926 (Construction) | Before exposure to hazards | Hazard communication, emergency action plans, fire prevention, PPE; must be documented |
| ADA Reasonable Accommodation | ADA Title I / 42 USC §12112 | Ongoing from hire | Interactive process upon request; onboarding must include accommodation intake mechanism |
| Anti-Harassment Training | CA Gov Code §12950.1; NY Labor Law §201-g; IL 775 ILCS 5/2-109 | Varies by state: within 6 months (NY); within first calendar year (CA); annual refresher | Track by state and hire date |
| HIPAA Privacy Training | 45 CFR §164.530(b) | Within reasonable period after hire | Required for covered entities and business associates; document completion |
| EEO-1 Data Collection | EEOC / 29 CFR 1602 | At hire | Voluntary self-ID of race/ethnicity and gender; used for annual EEO-1 filing (employers ≥100 employees) |

## KPIs

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| 90-day retention rate | (Remaining at 90 days ÷ Total new hires) × 100 | >90% |
| Onboarding completion rate | Completed tasks ÷ Assigned tasks × 100 | >95% |
| Training completion rate | Completed modules ÷ Required modules × 100 | 100% within 30 days |
| I-9 compliance rate | I-9s completed within 3 days ÷ Total hires × 100 | 100% (legal requirement) |
| New hire satisfaction score | Avg rating on 30- and 90-day surveys (1–5) | ≥4.0 |
| Hiring manager satisfaction | Manager rating of new hire readiness at 90 days (1–5) | ≥3.5 |

## Process

### Phase 0: Pre-Boarding (Offer Acceptance → Day 1)
1. Welcome email within 24 hours: start date logistics, parking/building access, dress code, Day 1 agenda, key contacts
2. Distribute compliance paperwork electronically: I-9 instructions, W-4, state withholding, direct deposit, benefits enrollment, NDA/IP assignment, handbook acknowledgment
3. Trigger IT provisioning: laptop, email, software licenses, VPN, badge; remote hires: ship equipment 2–3 business days before start
4. Assign onboarding buddy from outside new hire's immediate team
5. Send manager preparation checklist: draft 30/60/90-day goals, schedule Week 1 calendar, notify team, plan welcome lunch
6. Deliver welcome kit (physical or digital): swag, org chart, team directory, FAQ survival guide
7. Complete new hire reporting to state directory within 20 days per PRWORA

### Phase 1: Orientation (Day 1 → End of Week 1)
**Day 1:**
1. Greet personally — never let new hire sit alone waiting
2. Complete Form I-9 Section 2 within 3 business days of first day; do NOT specify which List A or List B/C documents to present
3. Company overview: mission, vision, values, strategic priorities, org structure, leadership introductions
4. Walk through handbook: PTO, code of conduct, anti-harassment, whistleblower, IT acceptable use
5. Benefits enrollment orientation: health/dental/vision/401(k)/FSA/HSA/life; note enrollment windows triggered by hire date
6. Workspace/technology setup: verify all system access, email, collaboration tools (Slack/Teams), HRIS self-service

**Days 2–5:**
7. Manager role overview: position description, team charter, key stakeholders, communication norms, recurring meetings
8. Peer introductions: 15–30 min 1:1s with 5–8 key collaborators (direct team, cross-functional, skip-level)
9. OSHA safety orientation: emergency exits, evacuation procedures, hazard communication, PPE if applicable
10. Buddy takes new hire to lunch or virtual coffee ≥2 times during Week 1
11. Manager delivers draft 30/60/90-day plan for collaborative refinement
12. End-of-week check-in: address questions, gauge emotional state, confirm Week 2 agenda

### Phase 2: Role-Specific Training (Weeks 2–4 / Days 6–30)
1. Execute structured training plan: self-paced LMS modules, instructor-led sessions, job shadowing with high performers, hands-on assignments
2. Assign initial "quick win" project — bounded, achievable deliverable building confidence and demonstrating value
3. Manager conducts twice-weekly 1:1s (30 min): progress vs. 30-day goals, questions/blockers, feedback on training, social integration check
4. Complete all mandatory compliance training: anti-harassment, data privacy/security, workplace safety, ethics/code of conduct
5. Introduce performance management system: goal-setting, review cadence, rating methodology, promotion criteria
6. **Day 30 milestone review**: manager and new hire formally assess 30-day goals; document progress; adjust 60-day targets; administer 30-day new hire satisfaction survey

### Phase 3: Integration (Days 31–90)
**Days 31–60 (Contributing Phase):**
1. Transition to active contribution: assign meaningful project ownership, include in decision-making, request input on team processes
2. Reduce 1:1 frequency to weekly; shift from "training review" to "performance coaching"
3. Expand cross-functional exposure: all-hands meetings, ERG/affinity groups, cross-departmental projects
4. Buddy relationship transitions to weekly or as-needed
5. **Day 60 checkpoint**: assess collaboration quality, technical competency growth, cultural adaptation; flag performance concerns to HR for early intervention

**Days 61–90 (Leading Phase):**
6. New hire takes initiative on independent work streams, proposes process improvements, mentors more recent hires if applicable
7. Manager collaboratively sets full-year performance goals
8. **Formal 90-day review**: assess against 30/60/90-day plan; discuss career development interests; confirm role fit; solicit candid onboarding feedback
9. Administer 90-day new hire survey (distinct from 30-day pulse)
10. HR debrief with manager: lessons learned, process improvements, update onboarding materials

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Pre-boarding | Offer acceptance → Day 1; administrative preparation, relationship initiation, anxiety reduction |
| Orientation | Days 1–5; organizational overview, policy review, compliance paperwork, workplace logistics |
| Onboarding | Full 90-day arc: orientation + training + integration |
| Four C's (SHRM) | Compliance, Clarification, Culture, Connection; defines onboarding maturity |
| 30-60-90-Day Plan | Learning (30 days) → Contributing (60 days) → Leading (90 days) |
| Time-to-Productivity (TTP) | Days from hire until employee reaches expected performance level |
| Onboarding Buddy | Peer outside direct team providing informal guidance and social integration support for 60–90 days |
| Form I-9 | Employment Eligibility Verification; employer examines documents within 3 business days; 8 CFR §274a.2 |
| E-Verify | USCIS web system comparing I-9 data to government records; mandatory for federal contractors |
| New Hire Reporting | PRWORA requirement to report new hires to state Directory of New Hires within 20 days |
| Ramp Period | Window during which new hire is learning and building capacity, not yet held to full performance standards |
| Quick Win | Bounded early project building new hire confidence and demonstrating value to team |
| HRIS | Human Resource Information System (Workday, BambooHR, ADP); manages records and triggers workflows |
| LMS | Learning Management System (Docebo, 360Learning, TalentLMS); delivers and tracks training |
| Net Onboarding Score (NOS) | NPS-style metric from new hire surveys on likelihood to recommend onboarding experience |
| Proactive Onboarding (Level 3) | SHRM's highest maturity level; all four C's systematically addressed; achieved by ~20% of organizations |
| Job Shadow | New hire observes high-performing employee executing real work tasks |
| Pulse Survey | 5–10 question survey at set intervals (Day 7, Day 30, Day 90) tracking new hire sentiment |
| Cognitive Load | Total mental effort for new information processing; exceeding capacity during orientation → information paralysis |
| Interactive Process (ADA) | Legally required employer-employee dialogue to identify and implement reasonable workplace accommodations |
| EVP | Employee Value Proposition; onboarding is the first test of whether EVP delivered is EVP promised |
| Preboarding Ghosting | No employer communication between offer acceptance and start; 28% of professionals have reneged on accepted offers |

## Checklist

- [ ] **I-9 Section 1 completed on or before first day; Section 2 completed within 3 business days — no exceptions, no document specification**
- [ ] **Federal W-4 and applicable state withholding certificate collected before first payroll run**
- [ ] **New hire report submitted to state Directory of New Hires within required timeline (20 days federally; check state deadlines)**
- [ ] Equipment, system access, email, and collaboration tools fully provisioned and tested before or on Day 1
- [ ] Onboarding buddy assigned and briefed on role, expectations, and time commitment before new hire's start date
- [ ] Hiring manager has drafted role-specific 30/60/90-day plan with measurable goals; reviewed with new hire by end of Week 1
- [ ] Mandatory compliance training (anti-harassment, safety, data privacy) assigned with deadlines and tracked in LMS
- [ ] **OSHA safety orientation completed before employee exposed to any workplace hazards — documented and filed**
- [ ] Benefits enrollment initiated within first week with clear deadline communication; follow-up reminder at midpoint of enrollment window
- [ ] Structured check-ins scheduled: daily in Week 1, twice-weekly Weeks 2–4, weekly Weeks 5–12; 1:1 with manager at each cadence
- [ ] 30-day new hire satisfaction pulse survey administered and results reviewed by HR and hiring manager
- [ ] Formal 90-day review conducted: performance against plan, cultural integration, career development, onboarding feedback captured
- [ ] **ADA accommodation intake mechanism included in pre-boarding workflow; requests routed to HR within 48 hours**
- [ ] All onboarding records (forms, training certificates, surveys, review documentation) stored in HRIS with appropriate retention schedules
- [ ] Onboarding program KPIs (retention at 90 days, time-to-productivity, completion rate, satisfaction score) calculated quarterly and reported to HR leadership
