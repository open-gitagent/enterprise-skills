## Role
Design and maintain governance frameworks that satisfy DGCL fiduciary standards, SEC/SOX disclosure obligations, and NYSE/NASDAQ listing rules while meeting institutional investor expectations.

## Fiduciary Duty Analysis Hierarchy

| Standard | Trigger | What Board Must Show |
|----------|---------|---------------------|
| Business Judgment Rule (BJR) | Default — disinterested, independent directors | Informed basis, good faith, honest belief in best interests |
| Enhanced Scrutiny | Defensive measures (Unocal 1985) or sale of control (Revlon 1986) | Reasonable investigation + proportionate/non-preclusive response |
| Entire Fairness | BJR rebutted: conflicts, self-dealing, controlling stockholder transactions | Fair dealing (process) AND fair price (substance) |
| Compelling Justification | Manipulation of corporate voting rights | Extremely high burden — rarely satisfied |
| Kahn v. M&F Worldwide (2014) restoration | Controlling stockholder merger | Independent special committee approval AND majority-of-minority stockholder vote restores BJR |

## Key Judicial Standards

| Case | Standard |
|------|---------|
| In re Caremark (Del. Ch. 1996) | Duty of oversight: Prong 1 = failure to implement compliance reporting system; Prong 2 = consciously ignoring red flags |
| Marchand v. Barnhill (Del. 2019) | Board liable where no committee charged with food safety oversight and no regular reports on mission-critical compliance |
| Kahn v. M&F Worldwide (Del. 2014) | Dual protection framework for controlling stockholder mergers restores BJR |
| Brewer v. Turner / Regions Financial (Del. Ch. Sept. 2025) | Caremark claim sustained where board delayed ending illegal overdraft practices |
| McDonald's (Del. Ch. 2023) | Officers owe independent Caremark duties to escalate red flags within their areas |

## Caremark Oversight Decision Tree

| Step | Question | If No → Risk |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Does board have compliance reporting system for mission-critical legal/regulatory areas? | Prong 1 exposure (Information System Claim) |
| 2 | Does board/committee receive regular reports on compliance risk areas? | Heightened exposure |
| 3 | When red flags surface, does board respond with documented deliberation? | Prong 2 exposure (Red Flags Claim) |
| 4 | Are officer-level escalation protocols defined? | Officer-level Caremark exposure (post-McDonald's) |

## Board Governance Maturity Scoring

| Dimension | Score 1 (Deficient) | Score 3 (Adequate) | Score 5 (Best Practice) |
|-----------|--------------------|--------------------|------------------------|
| Independence | Bare minimum compliance | Supermajority independent | All committees exceed minimums; lead independent director with defined role |
| Oversight Systems | No formal compliance reporting | Annual compliance presentations | Real-time dashboards; dedicated compliance committee; non-discretionary escalation protocols |
| Minutes Quality | Bare conclusions only | Substance of key discussions; vote outcomes | Deliberation detail evidencing process; conflict recusals noted; materials cross-referenced |
| Committee Charters | Boilerplate; not reviewed annually | Reviewed annually; exchange-compliant | Customized to risk profile; reviewed semi-annually; benchmarked against peers |
| Succession Planning | No formal plan | CEO succession reviewed annually | Comprehensive C-suite plan; emergency succession documented; board pipeline |
| Disclosure Compliance | Repeated SEC comments; late filings | Timely filings; minimal SEC comments | No SEC comments; proactive disclosure above minimums |

## Process

### 1. Board Composition & Independence Assessment
- Map each director against NYSE §303A.02 or Nasdaq Rule 5605(a)(2) independence criteria
- Apply heightened audit committee independence: Exchange Act Rule 10A-3 (no fees; not affiliated)
- Apply enhanced compensation committee factors: Exchange Act Rule 10C-1 (all compensatory relationships + affiliate status)
- Confirm majority-independent board requirement
- Document cooling-off periods: 3-year lookback for former employees (both NYSE and Nasdaq)
- Assess overboarding: ISS flags >4 public boards (or >2 if serving as public-company CEO)

### 2. Committee Charter Drafting & Review
- **Audit Committee**: auditor appointment/compensation/retention; pre-approval of services; complaint procedures; internal audit oversight (NYSE); related-party transaction review; authority to engage independent advisors
- **Compensation Committee**: CEO/executive pay determination; CEO excluded during deliberations on CEO pay; compensation consultant independence per Rule 10C-1; clawback policy administration; annual charter reassessment (Nasdaq)
- **Nom/Gov Committee**: director nominee criteria; annual board/committee self-evaluations; governance guidelines maintenance; succession planning oversight

### 3. Board Meeting Procedures & Minute Book
- Distribute packages ≥3–5 business days before meetings
- Minutes capture: date, time, location, attendees, quorum, comings/goings, substance of deliberations (not verbatim), motions with votes, recusals, adjournment
- Draft resolutions for all formal authorizations: equity issuances, officer appointments, material contracts, related-party transactions, dividends, compensation approvals
- Minute book contains: certificate of incorporation + amendments, bylaws + amendments, incorporator minutes, all stockholder and director meeting minutes/consents, committee minutes, officer/director resignations, stock ledger, foreign qualifications
- Destroy individual director notes after minutes approved; retain only final approved minutes and clean board packages

### 4. Annual Proxy & Disclosure Cycle
- File Schedule 14A with governance disclosures per Reg S-K Items 401–407
- Include independence determinations, board leadership structure, committee compositions, attendance records
- Pay-versus-performance disclosure per Item 402(v)
- File clawback policy as Exhibit 97 to Form 10-K; include cover-page restatement checkboxes
- Disclose insider trading policies and hedging/pledging restrictions per Item 408(b)
- Universal proxy card compliance for any contested elections per Rule 14a-19

### 5. SOX Compliance Integration
- CEO/CFO quarterly certifications per SOX §302
- Annual ICFR management assessment per SOX §404(a)
- External auditor attestation per SOX §404(b) where required
- Mandatory auditor lead engagement partner rotation every 5 years per SOX §203
- No personal loans to directors/officers per SOX §402

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Business Judgment Rule (BJR) | Judicial presumption that board decisions were informed, in good faith, in best interests; default standard |
| Caremark Claim | Derivative action for breach of oversight duty; Prong 1 = no compliance system; Prong 2 = ignored red flags |
| Entire Fairness | Most stringent review: fair dealing (process) AND fair price (substance); applies when BJR rebutted |
| Exculpation Clause | DGCL §102(b)(7): eliminates director/officer monetary liability for care breaches; not loyalty violations |
| Independent Director | No material relationship with company; definition varies across NYSE §303A.02, Nasdaq Rule 5605(a)(2), Rule 10A-3 |
| Audit Committee Financial Expert | Member meeting SEC criteria (Item 407(d)(5)): GAAP knowledge, experience preparing/auditing financials, familiarity with internal controls |
| Say-on-Pay | Non-binding advisory vote on exec compensation per Dodd-Frank §951; at least every 3 years |
| Universal Proxy Card | Required per Rule 14a-19 for contested elections; all nominees on single card |
| Dodd-Frank Clawback Policy | Mandatory recovery of incentive compensation from executive officers in 3 years preceding accounting restatement; filed as Exhibit 97 |
| Demand Futility | Plaintiff must plead particularized facts showing futility of demand on board to bring suit (Court of Chancery Rule 23.1) |
| Related-Party Transaction | Transaction between corporation and director/officer/controlling stockholder/affiliates; disclosed per Item 404 if >$120,000 |
| Overboarding | Director serving on too many boards; ISS flags >4 public boards or >2 if sitting CEO |
| Board Refreshment | Systematic process of adding new directors; evaluated through tenure analysis, evaluations, retirement age policies |
| Cooling-Off Period | Minimum time before former employee/auditor/adviser qualifies as independent; both exchanges: 3-year lookback |
| Written Consent | DGCL §228: stockholder/director action without a meeting with required vote signatures |
| Proxy Access | Stockholder right to nominate directors on company proxy card; common standard: 3% for 3 years → up to 20% of board |
| Enhanced Scrutiny | Intermediate standard for takeover defense (Unocal) and sale-of-control (Revlon) |
| Section 16 Reporting | Exchange Act §16: directors/officers/10%+ owners report on Forms 3/4/5; Form 4 within 2 business days |
| Corporate Governance Guidelines | NYSE §303A.09 required; covers qualifications, responsibilities, access, compensation, orientation, succession, evaluation |
| Material Weakness | ICFR deficiency with reasonable possibility that material misstatement won't be prevented/detected; must disclose per SOX §404 |
| Controlled Company | >50% voting power held by individual/group; exempt from majority-independent, independent compensation, and independent nom/gov requirements |
| D&O Insurance | Directors/officers liability; Side A (individual when company cannot indemnify); Side B (company indemnification reimbursement); Side C (entity securities claims) |

## Checklist

- [ ] Board independence verified under applicable exchange standard (NYSE §303A.02 OR Nasdaq Rule 5605(a)(2)) AND heightened audit committee standard (Rule 10A-3) AND compensation committee factors (Rule 10C-1) — each analysis documented separately
- [ ] All mandatory committees established with compliant composition: Audit (min. 3 independent + 1 financial expert), Compensation (all independent; Nasdaq min. 2), Nom/Gov (all independent or independent majority action)
- [ ] Committee charters reviewed and updated within last 12 months; references current requirements (cybersecurity, clawback, AI governance); formally readopted by board
- [ ] Minute book contains all required documents in chronological order
- [ ] Board resolutions exist for every action requiring formal authorization
- [ ] Dodd-Frank clawback policy adopted, filed as Exhibit 97 to Form 10-K; Form 10-K cover page includes restatement checkboxes
- [ ] SOX §302 certifications executed quarterly; §404 assessment completed annually
- [ ] Director conflict-of-interest procedures documented; recusal procedures reflected in minutes; related-party approval process delegated to audit committee
- [ ] Succession plan (planned and emergency) reviewed at least annually by full board or nom/gov; board skills matrix maintained
- [ ] Proxy statement disclosures complete per Reg S-K Items 401–408
- [ ] Caremark compliance infrastructure in place: board/committee receives at least quarterly reports on mission-critical compliance areas; non-discretionary escalation protocols exist; officer oversight responsibilities defined
- [ ] Section 16 filing procedures established; pre-clearance policy; Rule 10b5-1 plan procedures documented; Forms 3/4/5 filing responsibilities assigned; delinquent filing risk monitored
- [ ] Annual board and committee self-evaluation conducted; results documented; action items tracked
- [ ] Corporate governance guidelines adopted and publicly available with last revision date
