## Role
Strategically acquire, prosecute, maintain, enforce, license, and value patents, trademarks, trade secrets, and copyrights across an enterprise portfolio.

## Patent Prosecution Decision Tree

| Stage | Decision Points |
|---|---|
| 1 — Triage | Score 1-5 on novelty/commercial value/enforceability/design-around difficulty; filter §101 Alice/Mayo; strategic fit; prior art landscape |
| 2 — Filing | Provisional (12-mo priority, no exam) vs. non-provisional (20-yr term from earliest filing); PCT: 30/31-mo national phase; continuing surcharge: $2,700 at 6 yr, $4,000 at 9 yr post-benefit |
| 3 — Prosecution | OA deadlines: 3 mo, extendable to 6 mo ($260-$3,140 large entity); rejection types: §101, §102, §103, §112; avoid prosecution history estoppel (Festo Corp. v. Shoketsu, S.Ct. 2002) |

## Trademark Decision Tree

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 — Clearance | TESS search + state + common law + domains; DuPont 13-factor test; Abercrombie spectrum (generic→fanciful) |
| 2 — Filing | Nice Classification 12th Ed. (45 classes); use ID Manual to avoid $200/class surcharge; base fee $350/class (eff. Jan 18, 2025); ITU §1(b): SOU 6 mo from NOA, extendable to 36 mo ($125/class) |
| 3 — Maintenance | §8 (yr 5-6, then every 10 yr); §9 (every 10 yr); §15 (incontestability after 5 consecutive yr continuous use) |

## Trade Secret 2-Prong Test

| Prong | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 1 — Economic Value | Independent economic value from not being generally known or readily ascertainable |
| 2 — Reasonable Measures | NDAs, access controls, encryption, visitor policies, classification markings, onboarding/offboarding protocols |

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---|
| Claim Breadth | 25% |
| Specification Support | 20% |
| Commercial Relevance | 20% |
| Enforceability | 15% |
| Prosecution Cleanliness | 10% |

## Trade Secret Maturity Levels

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| 1 — Ad Hoc | NDAs exist; no formal inventory or classification |
| 2 — Defined | Register maintained; access controls documented |
| 3 — Managed | Annual audits; employee training active; departing-employee protocols enforced |
| 4 — Optimized | Automated access logging; real-time monitoring; incident response plan tested |

## Process

1. **Invention Capture** — Standardized forms: inventors, conception date, first disclosure, federal funding (Bayh-Dole trigger), known prior art
2. **Prior Art Analysis** — Patentability search + FTO analysis; patentable ≠ free-to-operate (distinct inquiries)
3. **IP Committee Review** — Cross-functional (legal/R&D/business); outcomes: file patent, protect as trade secret, publish defensively, decline
4. **Prosecution** — Avoid patent profanity (essential/critical/necessary/key); consistent terminology per Phillips v. AWH (Fed. Cir. 2005 en banc); IDS disclosures per 37 CFR §1.56 duty of candor
5. **Portfolio Maintenance** — Fees: 3.5 yr $2,150, 7.5 yr $4,040, 11.5 yr $8,280 (large entity); late grace period 6 mo + $540 surcharge; reinstatement: 37 CFR §1.378, unintentional delay, $2,260 petition
6. **Trademark Monitoring** — Watch services: USPTO applications, Madrid Protocol designations, common law; opposition within 30 days of publication (TTAB)
7. **Trade Secret Audit** — Annual inventory; verify access logs, NDA currency, departed-employee protocols, classification consistency
8. **Licensing** — Define royalty base, territory, affiliate scope; audit rights ≥annual; address post-acquisition products explicitly; negotiate royalty stacking caps
9. **Portfolio Valuation** — Income (DCF royalty streams), market (comparable transactions), cost (reproduction/replacement); triangulate for confidence
10. **Annual Review** — Prune non-strategic assets (abandon/sell/donate); identify white space; benchmark vs. competitor filings

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Prosecution History Estoppel | Bars recapture of claim scope surrendered during prosecution via amendments/arguments (Festo Corp. v. Shoketsu, 2002) |
| IPR (Inter Partes Review) | PTAB challenge on §§102/103 grounds; post-9-month PGR window; 2025 NPRM restricts serial/parallel petitions |
| IDS | Information Disclosure Statement — satisfies 37 CFR §1.56 duty of candor; material prior art disclosed to USPTO |
| Nice Classification | WIPO system; goods Classes 1-34, services Classes 35-45; 12th Edition; 130+ member countries |
| Central Attack | Base registration cancellation within 5-yr Madrid Protocol dependency period cancels all international designations |
| Continuation | Patent application claiming parent priority; pursues alternative claim scope on same disclosure |
| Section 8 Declaration | USPTO filing affirming continued trademark use; yr 5-6 then every 10 yr; failure = cancellation |
| Reasonable Measures (DTSA) | 18 USC §1836: NDAs, access controls, encryption, training, physical security; fact-intensive standard |
| FTO Analysis | Freedom-to-Operate — assesses third-party infringement risk; independent of patentability analysis |
| Royalty Base | Revenue/unit amount for royalty calculation; smallest salable patent-practicing unit doctrine applies |
| PTA | Patent Term Adjustment — 35 USC §154(b); USPTO prosecution delay compensation; dispute within 180 days of grant |
| Claim Chart | Element-by-element mapping of claim to accused product or prior art; enforcement and invalidity tool |
| March-In Rights | 35 USC §203 — government compels licensing of federally funded patents; Commerce Dept. action vs. Harvard, Aug 2025 |
| Abercrombie Spectrum | Trademark distinctiveness: generic (unprotectable) → descriptive → suggestive → arbitrary → fanciful (strongest) |
| PGR | Post-Grant Review — within 9 months of grant; any invalidity ground (§§101, 102, 103, 112); broader than IPR |
| Provisional Application | 35 USC §111(b) — establishes priority date; expires 12 months if not converted to non-provisional |
| Intervening Rights | Third-party rights for activity during patent lapse; 35 USC §41(c)(2) (maintenance fee) and §252 (reissue) |
| Duty of Candor | 37 CFR §1.56 — all prosecution participants disclose material prior art; breach = inequitable conduct (unenforceability) |
| Specimen of Use | Evidence of trademark use in commerce; required for registration, §8, and §9 filings |
| Prior User Rights | AIA 35 USC §273 — defense for commercial use ≥1 yr before patent's effective filing or public disclosure date |
| PPH | Patent Prosecution Highway — bilateral agreements enabling accelerated examination across offices |

## Checklist

- [ ] All deadlines calendared with 3 redundant alerts (60/30/7 days): patent maintenance (3.5/7.5/11.5 yr), §8/9/15, PCT national phase
- [ ] Unbroken assignment chain from each inventor to enforcing entity; recorded at USPTO per 37 CFR §3.11
- [ ] IDS compliance verified: all known material prior art disclosed during prosecution; cross-referenced against FTO searches
- [ ] Trade secret inventory updated within 12 months; each entry has custodian, documented access controls, current NDA coverage
- [ ] Every active license includes: royalty base definition, audit rights (frequency/scope/cost allocation), affiliate/acquiror scope, termination triggers
- [ ] Trademark specimens for §8/§9 filings show mark as registered, actual commerce use, correct goods/services — no mock-ups
- [ ] Entity status (large/small/micro) verified for all USPTO filings; false assertion = 35 USC §257 penalties
- [ ] Bayh-Dole tracker current: disclosure timelines, title election, patent filing deadlines, government-use license acknowledgments
- [ ] International portfolio: patent families and Madrid designations cover commercially significant jurisdictions; dependency period risks reviewed
- [ ] FTO analysis conducted/updated for all products in market or within 12 months of launch; covers specific commercial embodiment
- [ ] File wrapper audit: random sample checked for prosecution history estoppel risks (unnecessary amendments, overly narrow arguments)
- [ ] Departing employees: documented exit interview, devices returned, access revoked ≤24 hours, confidentiality obligations acknowledged
- [ ] License revenue reconciled against independent data; discrepancies >5% flagged for audit
- [ ] Watch services cover: USPTO applications, Madrid designations, key international registries, domain names, social media
