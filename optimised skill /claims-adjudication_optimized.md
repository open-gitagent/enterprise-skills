## Role
Insurance claims adjudication evaluates claims from FNOL through final disposition — payment, denial, or litigation — applying contract law, tort law, and regulatory compliance.

## Core Decision Framework

| Gate | Question | Key Actions |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 — Coverage Verification | Does coverage exist? | Confirm policy in force, claimant authorized, event within insuring agreement; identify all layers |
| 2 — Exclusion & Condition Analysis | Does anything negate coverage? | Evaluate exclusions narrowly; assess conditions precedent; apply state prejudice rules |
| 3 — Investigation & Liability | What happened, who is responsible? | Proportionate investigation; apply jurisdiction's negligence standard; calculate comparative fault |
| 4 — Damage Assessment & Reserves | What is this worth? | Categorize special/general damages; set case reserve (indemnity + ALAE + ULAE); update regularly |
| 5 — Resolution | How do we close? | Settlement, ADR, arbitration, or denial; tiered authority; denial must cite specific policy language per NAIC #900 §4(N) |

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: FNOL and Triage
1. Receive/log FNOL with date/time, claimant ID, loss description
2. Verify policy active on date of loss
3. Assign claim number; route by line, severity, complexity, jurisdiction
4. Apply automated red-flag scoring for SIU referral
5. Acknowledge receipt — NAIC #902 §5(A): within 10 working days (CA: 15 calendar days per 10 CCR §2695.5(e))
6. Issue reservation of rights letter if coverage questions exist

### Phase 2: Coverage Analysis
7. Pull complete policy file including endorsements, riders, amendments
8. Map loss to insuring agreements; identify all triggered coverages
9. Analyze each exclusion against known facts
10. Evaluate conditions precedent (notice, cooperation, proof of loss)
11. Research applicable state coverage interpretation law
12. Document coverage determination with policy citations; issue coverage position letter

### Phase 3: Investigation
13. Assign resources proportionate to complexity (staff, IA, or specialist)
14. Obtain recorded/written statements from insured, claimants, witnesses
15. Collect/preserve evidence, photos, police/fire reports, medical/financial records
16. Engage experts as needed (engineers, accountants, reconstructionists, origin-and-cause)
17. Conduct scene inspection where warranted
18. Refer to SIU if fraud indicators emerge
19. Evaluate subrogation potential

### Phase 4: Liability Determination (Third-Party)
20. Analyze applicable standard of care and duty
21. Apply jurisdiction negligence framework (pure comparative / modified comparative / contributory)
22. Calculate fault allocation among all parties
23. Assess joint-and-several vs. proportionate responsibility
24. Evaluate statutory immunities, caps, limitations periods
25. Document liability determination with evidence citations

### Phase 5: Damage Assessment
26. Property: contractor estimates, Xactimate/Symbility, depreciation schedules, ACV vs. RCV
27. Bodily injury: medical records/bills, lost wages, permanency/impairment, general damages via verdict research
28. Commercial: business interruption, extra expense, contingent BI, period of restoration
29. Set initial case reserve (probable ultimate cost including ALAE)
30. Review reserve adequacy at 30/60/90-day cycles

### Phase 6: Settlement & Resolution
31. Prepare settlement evaluation memo (coverage, liability, damages, disposition)
32. Obtain settlement authority at appropriate tier
33. Analyze claimant demand against independent valuation
34. Present offer with documented rationale
35. Explore ADR (mediation, arbitration, appraisal) where appropriate
36. Tender payment within 30 days of liability affirmation per NAIC #902 §6(F)
37. Denials: cite specific policy provisions and factual basis per NAIC #900 §4(N)
38. Pursue subrogation post-settlement
39. Close file with complete documentation

## Evaluation Criteria

**Timeliness**
- FNOL acknowledgment: 10 working days (NAIC) or state-specific
- Initial claimant contact: 24–48 hours of assignment
- Coverage determination: 15 business days of complete information
- Payment after affirmation: 30 days per NAIC #902 §6(F)
- Texas Ch. 542B: acknowledge 15 days, accept/reject 15 business days, pay 5 business days

**Coverage Analysis**
- Every denial cites specific policy language
- All endorsements reviewed and applied
- State-mandated coverages confirmed (UM/UIM, PIP, medpay)
- ROR issued before coverage-questioning investigation

**Investigation**
- Proportionate to claim size/complexity
- All material witnesses contacted and documented
- Relevant expert opinions obtained
- Evidence preservation followed
- SIU referrals include specific indicators

**Reserve Accuracy**
- Initial reserve within 30 days of assignment
- Reflects probable ultimate cost (indemnity + ALAE)
- Minimum quarterly review for open claims
- Reserve-to-paid ratio within acceptable ranges by line

**Settlement**
- Within range of documented valuation
- Authority obtained before offer extended
- Verdict/settlement research documented
- No coercive tactics per NAIC #900 §4

## Red Flags & Edge Cases

1. **Late-reported claims-made claims**: Analyze extended reporting period, whether claim was first made during policy period, and state prejudice rules for claims-made vs. occurrence late notice.
2. **Concurrent causation**: Determine if jurisdiction follows efficient proximate cause doctrine or enforces ACC clause (cf. post-Katrina precedent).
3. **Stacking/anti-stacking**: Confirm whether jurisdiction permits UM/UIM stacking and anti-stacking endorsement enforceability (PA, FL have specific statutes).
4. **Time-limited demands**: Strict compliance required; GA Pierce v. Banks (2023) — missing comma voided demand acceptance.
5. **Algorithmic denial without human review**: Violates NAIC AI Model Bulletin human oversight requirement and NAIC #900 §4(C).
6. **Labor depreciation in ACV**: Challenged in class actions; multiple states prohibit by statute.
7. **Appraisal delay tactics**: Courts scrutinize insurer waiver-by-conduct in appraisal timing disputes.
8. **Vague ROR letters**: Must cite specific provisions; boilerplate may estop coverage defenses.
9. **Unsupported SIU referral as delay**: Audited in market conduct; low referral-to-finding ratios trigger enforcement; NAIC #900 §4(C).
10. **Excess carrier notice failure**: Late notice → excess denial → insured exposed → bad faith risk for primary.
11. **Post-loss rescission**: State rules vary on intent vs. materiality; contestability limits apply (e.g., NY).
12. **Total-loss auto valuation**: NAIC #902 §8 requires fair market value with documented local comparable vehicles (CCC, Mitchell, Audatex).
13. **Cross-coverage settlement manipulation**: Prohibited by NAIC #900 §4(D); adopted by 30+ states.
14. **Business interruption period-of-restoration**: Apply "reasonable speed" standard; document factors (supply chain, permitting).

## Common Mistakes

1. Denial letters paraphrase exclusions instead of quoting exact policy text
2. Failing to re-investigate after new information received
3. Setting reserves to adjuster authority level, not probable ultimate cost
4. Post-hoc denial rationale created during litigation vs. contemporaneous documentation
5. Applying company-wide comparative negligence standard without jurisdiction-specific rules
6. Treating first-party insured adversarially — creates bad faith exposure
7. Overlooking additional insureds/interests on endorsements
8. Using biased IME vendors with uniform insurer-favorable findings
9. Issuing payment without confirming correct payee/mortgage clause
10. Ignoring subrogation recovery opportunities

## Regulatory & Compliance Requirements

### Federal Framework
| Law | Key Point |
|-----|-----------|
| McCarran-Ferguson Act (15 U.S.C. §§1011-1015) | State regulation preserved; federal applies only where state does not |
| ERISA (29 U.S.C. §1001 et seq.) | Preempts state law for self-funded plans |
| NFIP (42 U.S.C. §4001 et seq.) | FEMA standards; outside state unfair claims acts |

### NAIC Models
| Model | Key Requirements |
|-------|-----------------|
| #900 — Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act (1990/1997) | 14 prohibited practices §4; penalties $1,000/violation, $25,000/flagrant; caps $100,000/$250,000; license revocation possible |
| #902 — P&C Claims Regulation (1990/1997) | Acknowledge 10 days §5A; investigate 10 days §5B; tender 30 days §6F; total-loss valuation §8; RCV standards §9 |
| #903 — Life/A&H Claims Regulation | Parallel standards for life/health |
| #910 — Market Conduct Record Retention | Retain claim files/correspondence for examination |

## Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| FNOL | Initial loss report; starts all regulatory timelines |
| ROR (Reservation of Rights) | Written notice preserving coverage defenses; must cite specific provisions |
| Subrogation | Insurer's right to recover from responsible third party post-payment |
| ACV | Replacement cost minus depreciation; broad evidence rule in majority of states |
| RCV | Repair/replace cost without depreciation deduction; holdback released after repair |
| ALAE | Allocated loss adjustment expenses — claim-specific costs (experts, defense, court) |
| ULAE | Unallocated LAE — overhead allocated actuarially across book |
| Bad Faith | Breach of good faith/fair dealing; statutory, contractual, or tortious; remedies vary by state |
| SIU | Special Investigations Unit; fraud referral triggers state fraud bureau reporting |
| Proof of Loss | Sworn insured statement of facts/amount; typically due within 60 days |
| EUO | Examination Under Oath; under cooperation clause; failure may void coverage |
| Comparative Negligence | Pure / modified 50%–51% bar / contributory (DC, MD, VA, AL, NC) |
| Occurrence | Date-of-event trigger for occurrence-based policies |
| Claims-Made Policy | Covers claims first made and reported during policy period; tail/ERP issues |
| Appraisal | Amount-of-loss ADR: each party selects appraiser; umpire breaks tie |
| Loss Reserve | Case reserves + IBNR; actuarially certified; regulatory reviewed |
| Duty to Defend | Broader than indemnity; triggered by potentially covered allegations |
| Estoppel/Waiver | Bars insurer from asserting previously waived or prejudicially abandoned defenses |
| Tender of Defense | Formal insured request for insurer to assume defense; late tender may prejudice coverage |
| Stacking | Combining multiple policy limits; permitted/prohibited varies by state |
| Market Conduct Examination | State DOI audit of claims practices; findings trigger fines, corrective action |
| Period of Restoration | BI coverage period from physical loss to reasonable-speed repair completion |
| Advance Payment | Partial pre-settlement payment; per NAIC #902 §6(D) not a waiver or release |
| ACC Clause | Excludes coverage when covered and excluded perils concurrently cause loss; enforceability varies |

## Quality Checklist

1. All policy forms, endorsements, amendments on date of loss reviewed and documented
2. Regulatory timelines verified against governing state (not just NAIC model)
3. Denial cites exact policy provision (section, paragraph, endorsement number) and factual basis
4. Case reserve reflects probable ultimate cost (indemnity + ALAE), not authority limits; rationale documented
5. Investigation scope matches claim complexity and dollar exposure
6. ROR issued before investigation conducted under insured's policy when coverage in question
7. Subrogation rights identified, documented, preserved (lien notice, evidence, statute of limitations)
8. SIU referrals include specific documented red-flag indicators
9. Settlement offers/payments approved at proper authority level before communicating to claimant
10. Excess/umbrella carriers notified timely for claims potentially exceeding primary limits
11. All communications logged with date, time, participants, substance; diary maintained
12. Fault allocation uses correct jurisdiction-specific negligence standard; documented with evidence
13. Human review confirmed for any automated triage, valuation, or denial per NAIC AI Bulletin
14. Claim file stands alone as complete narrative without oral explanation
15. Claim status communications clear, timely, compliant with consumer protection standards
