## Role
Design and administer performance management systems covering goal architecture, continuous feedback, calibration, and outcome linkage to drive employee performance and legal compliance.

## System Design Decision Framework

| Layer | Decision | Key Parameters |
|---|---|---|
| Philosophy | Evaluation vs. development orientation | Separate conversations ≥4-6 weeks apart; balance both for 4.2× performance advantage (McKinsey) |
| Goal Architecture | Cascade vs. Align vs. Emerge | Cascade=MBO/hierarchical; Align=OKR bidirectional (60% bottom-up per Google re:Work); Emerge=sprint/agile; most use hybrid |
| Feedback Architecture | Cadence × Formality × Direction | Cadence: weekly 1:1/monthly check-in/quarterly formal/annual evaluation; Direction: manager/peer/upward/360/self |
| Rating Methodology | Scale × Distribution × Anchoring | 3/4/5-point or no-rating; open/guided/forced distribution; BARS/competency/results/blended anchors |
| Outcome Linkage | Performance → Decisions | Compensation via merit matrix + compa-ratio; promotion = performance + readiness + availability; IDP 70/20/10; PIP 30-90 days |

## Rating Scale (Recommended 5-Point with Behavioral Anchors)

| Rating | Label | Definition | Guideline Distribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | Exceptional | Exceeds all objectives; enterprise-wide impact; role-model behaviors | 5-10% |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Exceeds most objectives; impact beyond role; strong behavioral alignment | 20-25% |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Fully achieves objectives; consistent competencies | 40-50% |
| 2 | Partially Meets | Some objectives achieved; inconsistent competencies; development needed | 15-20% |
| 1 | Does Not Meet | Fails minimum standards; immediate improvement required | 0-5% |

## Calibration Effectiveness Standards

| Criterion | Standard |
|---|---|
| Rating distribution within guideline | ±5% |
| Protected class rating disparity | Four-fifths (80%) rule compliant |
| Ratings >4 or <2 | Documented evidence from multiple sources |
| Session duration | 2-4 hours per 50 employees reviewed |
| Minimum rating changes per session | ≥3 (indicates genuine calibration) |

## Process

**Phase 1 — System Design (Months 1-3)**
1. Conduct stakeholder interviews: senior leaders, managers at 3+ levels, employee focus groups
2. Audit current state: completion rates, rating distributions by dept/demographics, outcome correlation, time-to-complete
3. Select philosophy, goal architecture, feedback cadence, rating methodology, outcome linkage
4. Design forms/rubrics/conversation guides — behavioral anchors job-related and validated per Uniform Guidelines 29 CFR §1607.14
5. Configure platform (Workday/SAP SuccessFactors/Oracle HCM/Lattice/15Five/BetterWorks/Culture Amp/Reflektive): workflow approvals, notifications, data access, reporting
6. Run adverse impact analysis of proposed rating scale using four-fifths (80%) rule across race, gender, age, disability from historical data

**Phase 2 — Manager Enablement (Months 3-4)**
7. Train managers: goal-setting (SMART-Plus), feedback (SBI model: Situation-Behavior-Impact), coaching, bias (halo/recency/central tendency/affinity/horn/contrast/leniency/severity)
8. Train HR business partners: calibration facilitation, bias intervention, documentation standards
9. Distribute toolkits: conversation starters, difficult feedback language, documentation templates

**Phase 3 — Goal Setting (Annual/Semi-Annual Kickoff)**
10. Communicate organizational and BU objectives with rationale
11. Employees draft 3-5 objectives with measurable key results and target dates; manager review within 2 weeks
12. Document goals in system of record; both parties sign off digitally
13. Identify ADA reasonable accommodations for goal-setting and review participation

**Phase 4 — Continuous Feedback (Ongoing)**
14. Weekly/biweekly 1:1s: goal progress, obstacles/support, feedback exchange, development activities
15. Real-time recognition/feedback in platform (peer kudos, manager notes)
16. Quarterly check-ins: formal goal progress review, mid-course adjustments documented, new priorities incorporated

**Phase 5 — Formal Review (Semi-Annual or Annual)**
17. Employee completes self-assessment against goals and competencies
18. Manager drafts evaluation: documented feedback throughout cycle, goal data, peer/stakeholder input, self-assessment
19. Manager's manager reviews draft for consistency before delivery
20. Face-to-face review ≥45 minutes; manager presents, invites employee response, discusses development

**Phase 6 — Calibration (Post-Draft, Pre-Final)**
21. HR prepares data: proposed ratings by team, demographic breakdowns, historical comparison, outlier ID
22. Facilitate calibration by BU: managers present evidence; peers challenge; HR monitors for bias
23. Apply four-fifths rule to proposed final ratings across all protected classes
24. Finalize ratings; communicate adjustments with documented rationale

**Phase 7 — Outcome Execution (Post-Calibration)**
25. Generate merit increase recommendations using performance-compa-ratio matrix
26. Identify promotion candidates: high performance + demonstrated readiness + role availability
27. Create IDPs for all employees: specific actions, timelines, resource commitments; 70/20/10 allocation
28. Issue PIPs where required: specific deficiencies, measurable targets, support, review dates, consequences; any PIP within 90 days of protected activity requires independent HR and legal review
29. Archive: EEOC minimum 1yr post-separation (29 CFR §1602.14); OFCCP 2yr (41 CFR §60-1.12); best practice 7yr

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| OKR | Objectives and Key Results; 60-70% achievement = appropriate stretch; originated Intel, popularized Google |
| Calibration Session | Cross-manager meeting to standardize ratings against common criteria; HR-facilitated; reduces inter-rater variability |
| Forced Distribution (Stack Ranking) | Predetermined % at each level (e.g., 20/70/10); legal risk + talent attrition (Cornell 2025); declining use |
| PIP | Performance Improvement Plan; specifies deficiencies, targets, support, timeline 30-90 days, consequences |
| BARS | Behaviorally Anchored Rating Scale; specific behavioral examples per level; reduces halo/central tendency bias |
| Recency Bias | Overweighting recent events vs. full evaluation period; mitigated by quarterly documentation |
| Halo Effect | Single positive attribute inflates ratings across unrelated dimensions |
| Central Tendency Bias | Clustering ratings near midpoint; avoids extremes; prevalent in 5-point scales and harmony cultures |
| Four-Fifths (80%) Rule | Protected group selection rate <80% of highest-scoring group = adverse impact presumed (EEOC Uniform Guidelines) |
| 9-Box Grid | Performance (x) × potential (y) matrix for succession; criticized for subjective potential introducing demographic bias |
| Compa-Ratio | Current salary ÷ pay range midpoint; used in merit matrices to ensure market equity |
| SBI Model | Situation-Behavior-Impact feedback framework; factual observation, not character judgment |
| Skip-Level Review | Conversation between employee and manager's manager; validates ratings, surfaces hidden talent |
| IDP | Individual Development Plan; 70/20/10 allocation (experience/relationships/formal training); future focus |
| 360-Degree Feedback | Multi-rater from direct reports/peers/manager/stakeholders; best for development not evaluation |
| Merit Matrix | Maps performance rating × compa-ratio → merit increase percentage |
| Continuous Performance Management (CPM) | Ongoing goal tracking + real-time feedback + check-ins; adopted Adobe/Microsoft/IBM post-2015 |
| Progressive Discipline | Verbal → written → final warning → termination for conduct; distinct from performance management |
| Rater Reliability | Consistency across raters evaluating same employee; low = measuring manager variance not performance |
| Leniency/Severity Bias | Systematic rating all employees higher (leniency) or lower (severity) than warranted |
| Competency Model | Organization-specific knowledge/skills/abilities/behaviors by level; defines "how" of performance |
| Talent Review | Quarterly/semi-annual leadership meeting on succession pipelines, HiPo investment, capability gaps |
| Sandbagging | Setting easy goals to guarantee achievement; indicates over-linking goal achievement to compensation |
| At-Will Employment | US termination-without-cause principle; performance documentation provides legitimate business reason |

## Checklist

- [ ] All performance criteria job-related from validated job descriptions or competency models
- [ ] Adverse impact analysis (four-fifths rule) run on final ratings across race, gender, age (40+), disability — documented and reviewed by employment counsel
- [ ] Calibration sessions for every BU with HR facilitation before ratings finalized
- [ ] Every employee rated below "Meets Expectations" has documented evidence from multiple touchpoints throughout cycle
- [ ] Every PIP includes: written specific deficiencies, measurable targets, explicit support/resources, defined timeline, documented check-in dates
- [ ] Performance and compensation conversations separated by ≥4 weeks
- [ ] Non-exempt employees compensated for time on self-assessments, review meetings, goal-setting (FLSA 29 USC §201)
- [ ] Manager training on bias recognition and feedback delivery completed before review cycle; attendance documented
- [ ] Reasonable accommodation requests for review process (format/timeline/documentation) addressed via interactive process
- [ ] Rating scale has behavioral anchors specific to job family or level (not generic descriptors)
- [ ] Goals reviewed and updated ≥once mid-cycle (not set-and-forget)
- [ ] Documentation retention enforced: EEOC ≥1yr post-separation (29 CFR §1602.14); OFCCP 2yr (41 CFR §60-1.12); best practice 7yr
- [ ] Employee acknowledgment of review receipt captured (signature or digital confirmation)
- [ ] Employee right-of-response mechanism available: written rebuttal attached to formal review
- [ ] Post-cycle effectiveness metrics tracked: completion rates, on-time rates, rating distribution variance, voluntary turnover correlation, employee satisfaction
