## Role
Align workforce supply (quantity, quality, location, cost) with business strategy over a 3-5 year horizon through driver-based demand modeling, skills gap analysis, succession planning, and 6B intervention portfolios.

## Diagnose → Project → Gap → Act Cycle

| Phase | Decision | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Strategy Decode | Translate strategy → workforce implications by job family, geography, capability | Map each strategic priority: value-creating roles / enabling roles / declining roles |
| 2 — Supply Assessment | Internal labor supply: headcount, skills, tenure, performance, diversity, attrition | Markov chain / transition probability matrices for promotions/laterals/attrition/retirements over horizon |
| 3 — Demand Projection | Driver-based FTE demand per scenario | 3-4 scenarios: baseline / optimistic / pessimistic / disruptive (tech substitution or regulatory); NOT headcount extrapolation |
| 4 — Gap Quantification | Quantity (FTE surplus/deficit) + Quality (skill mix) + Timing + Cost gaps | Criticality matrix: business impact (revenue at risk) × difficulty to fill (market scarcity, time to proficiency) |
| 5 — 6B Strategy | Build / Buy / Borrow / Bot / Bind / Bounce | TCOW modeled for each lever scenario |
| 6 — Org Design | Spans, layers, reporting logic | McKinsey archetypes: player/coach 3-5 reports; coordinator 15+; benchmark ≤5 layers CEO to frontline |

## Workforce Plan Quality Scorecard

| Dimension | Weight | Key Indicators |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic Alignment | 25% | Every strategic initiative mapped to workforce implications; validated by CEO/CFO |
| Analytical Rigor | 20% | Multiple scenarios; driver-based demand; Markov supply model calibrated to 3-year historical data |
| Gap Specificity | 15% | Gaps quantified by job family, skill, location, timeline — not aggregate FTE |
| Action Feasibility | 15% | Each intervention: owner, timeline, budget, success metric; reskilling plans have curricula |
| Financial Integration | 10% | TCOW reconciled with CFO's financial plan; ROI estimated for build/buy/borrow |
| Risk Mitigation | 10% | Adverse impact analysis; WARN Act exposure; single-point-of-failure roles identified |
| Governance | 5% | Quarterly review cadence; data refresh process; steering committee charter |

## KPI Benchmarks

| KPI | Target |
|---|---|
| Workforce Plan Accuracy | Actual vs. planned headcount ±5% at 12-month mark |
| Critical Role Vacancy Rate | <5% unfilled >90 days |
| Succession Coverage Ratio | ≥2 ready-now or ready-in-1-year candidates per critical role |
| Internal Fill Rate | ≥60% of roles above IC filled internally |
| Revenue per FTE | Technology ~$300K; Healthcare ~$150K; Retail ~$150K |
| Regrettable Attrition | Below industry median for target talent segments |

## Regulatory Framework

| Law | Citation | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Federal WARN Act | 29 USC §§2101-2109 | 60-day notice for plant closing (50+ employees at single site) or mass layoff (50-499 if ≥33% workforce, or 500+); employment losses aggregated within any 90-day period; penalties: back pay + benefits up to 60 days + $500/day |
| State mini-WARN | NY: 90 days; CA: Cal-WARN | Stricter notice periods |
| Title VII + EEOC UGESP | 42 USC §2000e; 29 CFR Part 1607 | Adverse impact analysis on all selection procedures including layoffs; four-fifths (80%) rule |
| ADEA + OWBPA | 29 USC §621; 29 USC §626(f) | Heightened scrutiny for RIFs affecting 40+; group terminations require 45-day consideration period, 7-day revocation, decisional unit demographics disclosure |
| OFCCP | 41 CFR Parts 60-1/60-2/60-3 | Federal contractors 50+ employees + $50K+ contracts: AAP for women/minorities/veterans/disabled; adverse impact analysis on all workforce planning actions; 2024 AI guidance for workforce tools |
| ADA Title I | 42 USC §12101 | Restructuring cannot eliminate disabled employees' positions as pretext; reassignment to vacant positions = reasonable accommodation |

## Process

**Phase 1 — Strategic Alignment (Weeks 1-3)**
1. Obtain board-approved strategic plan, capital plan, 3-5 year financial projections
2. C-suite/BU leader stakeholder interviews → decode strategic priorities into workforce implications
3. Define planning horizon (3 years operating; 5 years transformation)
4. Establish cross-functional steering committee: CHRO, CFO, COO, BU heads
5. Agree on planning segments (job families, locations, BUs, cost centers)

**Phase 2 — Current State Diagnosis (Weeks 3-6)**
6. Extract headcount from HRIS: FTE, contingent, open positions by segment
7. Build skills inventory: HRIS skills fields + learning completions + certifications + manager assessments or AI-inferred skills
8. Analyze attrition: voluntary turnover by tenure band; regrettable vs. non-regrettable; root cause from exit data
9. Model internal supply via transition matrices (promotion rates, mobility rates, retirement eligibility 1/3/5 years)
10. Assess org health: spans distribution, layer count, manager-to-IC ratio, revenue per FTE vs. benchmarks

**Phase 3 — Demand Modeling (Weeks 6-9)**
11. Identify workload drivers per BU (e.g., policies per underwriter, patients per clinician, tickets per agent)
12. Model FTE demand under 3-4 scenarios using driver-based formulas
13. Map skill demand per scenario using EMSI/Lightcast, LinkedIn Talent Insights, O*NET
14. Quantify automation displacement: which tasks within roles are automatable and by when
15. Validate with BU leaders via structured workshops

**Phase 4 — Gap Analysis (Weeks 9-11)**
16. Quantity gaps (FTE surplus/deficit) by segment and scenario
17. Quality gaps (skill surplus/deficit): criticality × current proficiency × market availability
18. Identify critical role vacancies with single points of failure
19. Map succession pipeline for top 50-100 critical roles: 9-box grid (performance × potential); readiness: ready now / 1-2 years / 3+ years; target ≥2 ready-now successors per critical role
20. Adverse impact analysis on planned reductions using four-fifths rule per 29 CFR Part 1607

**Phase 5 — Action Planning (Weeks 11-14)**
21. Build 6B intervention portfolio with cost-benefit estimates
22. Develop reskilling roadmaps for priority gaps (target 12-18 month proficiency timelines)
23. Design succession acceleration plans for roles with <2 ready-now successors
24. Org design recommendations: layer reduction targets, span normalization, role consolidation
25. Model TCOW under each scenario: base salary + benefits + contingent + reskilling + severance
26. Stress-test against worst-case scenario and budget constraints

**Phase 6 — Execution & Monitoring (Ongoing)**
27. Integrate into annual operating plan and budget cycle
28. Quarterly workforce planning reviews with steering committee
29. Track leading indicators: pipeline fill rates, internal mobility rate, time-to-fill critical roles, training completion, regrettable attrition
30. Refresh scenario assumptions quarterly
31. Annual full refresh with updated strategy inputs

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| 6B Framework | Build/Buy/Borrow/Bot/Bind/Bounce; strategic levers for closing workforce gaps |
| 9-Box Grid | Performance (x) × Potential (y) matrix for talent classification; from "manage out" to "future leader" |
| Adverse Impact Analysis | Statistical assessment of employment decisions on protected classes; four-fifths rule or Fisher's exact/Z-test |
| Critical Role | Top 3-5% of positions where vacancy creates disproportionate business risk |
| Driver-Based Demand | Links FTE requirements to measurable business drivers (revenue/FTE, tickets/agent, patients/nurse) vs. historical extrapolation |
| Four-Fifths Rule | EEOC: selection rate for protected group <80% of highest-selected group = adverse impact presumed |
| Internal Mobility Rate | % open positions filled internally; best-in-class ≥60% for roles above entry level |
| Markov Chain Analysis | Statistical method modeling workforce transition probabilities (promotions/exits/retirements) from historical data |
| Readiness Rating | Ready now (<30 days); ready 1-2 years (specific development needed); emerging (3+ year trajectory) |
| Regrettable Attrition | Voluntary departure of employees org wanted to retain; excludes low performers/role eliminations |
| Revenue per FTE | Total revenue ÷ avg FTE; Technology ~$300K; SaaS $130K-$300K by stage; Retail/Healthcare ~$150K |
| Scenario Planning | Multi-scenario modeling with trigger-based decision points; Deloitte 2025: foundational to modern SWP |
| Skills Gap Analysis | Systematic comparison of current capabilities vs. future requirements → prioritized deficit inventory |
| Skills Taxonomy | Structured skill classification (ESCO/O*NET/Lightcast); foundation of skills-based workforce planning |
| Span of Control | Direct reports per manager; complex/strategic: 3-5 (McKinsey player/coach); standardized: 12-15+ (coordinator) |
| Succession Pipeline | Inventory of developing candidates per critical role; measured by depth and readiness |
| SWP | Strategic Workforce Planning; continuous process aligning workforce supply with business demand 3-5 years |
| TCOW | Total Cost of Workforce: base + benefits + variable + contingent + reskilling + relocation + severance |
| WARN Act Aggregation | 90-day rolling window for cumulating employment losses at single site per 29 USC §2101 |
| Workforce Digital Twin | AI-powered live simulation of workforce data; test restructuring/automation scenarios before implementation |
| ZBWP | Zero-Based Workforce Planning; every role justified from scratch; powerful but risks institutional knowledge loss |

## Checklist

- [ ] Business strategy decoded into specific workforce implications by job family, skill, and location — not aggregate headcount target
- [ ] Supply model uses segmented attrition rates (by tenure, performance, job family) — not single blended turnover %
- [ ] Demand model is driver-based (workload drivers × productivity assumptions) — not headcount extrapolation
- [ ] At least three scenarios modeled (baseline, upside, downside) with explicit documented assumptions
- [ ] Skills gap analysis references external labor market data (Lightcast, O*NET, LinkedIn Talent Insights) to validate fill feasibility
- [ ] Succession pipeline covers all critical roles with ≥2 candidates and readiness levels validated through calibration sessions
- [ ] Org design analysis: spans-of-control distribution, layer count, manager-to-IC ratio benchmarked vs. industry
- [ ] TCOW modeled and reconciled with CFO's financial plan and budget assumptions
- [ ] Adverse impact analysis on planned reductions/restructurings using four-fifths rule per 29 CFR Part 1607
- [ ] WARN Act exposure assessed: any scenario with 50+ employment losses at single site within 90-day window per 29 USC §2101
- [ ] Contingent workforce included in planning scope with cost, skill, and risk implications
- [ ] Reskilling plans for priority gaps: specific curricula, timelines, success metrics, budget allocations
- [ ] Plan validated by cross-functional steering committee (HR, Finance, Operations, BU leaders)
- [ ] Quarterly review cadence and refresh triggers documented with named accountable owners
- [ ] Automation/AI displacement modeled at task level within affected roles — not just whole-role elimination
