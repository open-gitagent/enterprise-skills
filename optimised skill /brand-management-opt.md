## Role
Govern enterprise brand portfolio across architecture, governance, equity measurement, and crisis response to protect and grow brand as a measurable financial asset.

## Decision Framework

| Domain | Decision Logic |
|--------|---------------|
| Architecture Selection | Branded House = efficiency + concentrated risk; House of Brands = isolation + multiplied cost; weigh audience overlap, reputational risk correlation, M&A cost tolerance, brand stretch credibility |
| Governance Intensity | Centralized = pharma/financial services; Federated = global CPG; 2025 trend: "adaptive governance" with automated compliance at point of creation |
| Equity Investment Allocation | IPA recommendation: 60:40 brand-to-activation ratio; under-investing in brand yields diminishing returns within 6–12 months |
| Crisis Severity | Minor = monitor only; Moderate = communications playbook; Severe (C-suite scandal, data breach, deaths) = full War Room + board involvement |

## Architecture Models

| Model | Example | Strength | Risk |
|-------|---------|---------|------|
| Branded House | Apple, Google | Equity efficiency | Crisis contaminates all |
| Sub-Brands | Microsoft Azure/365 | Dual recognition | Complex management |
| Endorsed Brands | Marriott Courtyard | Credibility transfer | Limited sub-brand independence |
| House of Brands | P&G (Tide, Pampers, Gillette) | Full risk isolation | Multiplied investment |
| Hybrid | Coca-Cola Company | Flexibility | Architecture complexity |

## Brand Equity Valuation by Purpose

| Purpose | Framework |
|---------|-----------|
| M&A / Licensing | Interbrand methodology (ISO 10668): economic earnings → Role of Brand Index → brand strength discount |
| Strategic Planning | Keller CBBE: map across pyramid levels; identify weakest level as investment priority |
| Competitive Benchmarking | BrandZ brand contribution or Brand Finance royalty relief |
| Operational Management | Aaker 5-component dashboard: awareness, quality, associations, loyalty, proprietary assets |

## Regulatory Requirements

| Requirement | Obligation |
|-------------|-----------|
| Lanham Act (15 U.S.C. §1051) | Governs trademark registration, protection, enforcement; §43(a) prohibits false designations and false advertising |
| USPTO Maintenance | Section 8 Declaration due years 5–6 post-registration; combined Section 8/9 Renewal due years 9–10 and every 10 years; missed = cancellation |
| Madrid Protocol | International trademark registration across 130+ countries via single application |
| FTC Act §5 (15 U.S.C. §45) | Prohibits unfair/deceptive acts including misleading brand claims |
| FTC Endorsement Guides 16 CFR Part 255 (2023) | Disclose material connections for influencer/employee/testimonial programs; penalties up to $50,120 per incident |
| ISO 10668:2010 | Standard for monetary brand valuation; Interbrand certified compliant |
| ISO 20671:2019 | Non-monetary brand evaluation principles and fundamentals |
| GDPR Articles 6 & 7 | Lawful processing/consent for brand communications targeting EU data subjects |
| CAN-SPAM Act (15 U.S.C. §7701) | Commercial email: accurate headers, honest subjects, opt-out, physical address; penalties up to $51,744 per email |
| CCPA/CPRA (Cal. Civ. Code §1798.100) | Consumer privacy rights affecting brand data collection and targeted advertising |
| ADA Title III / WCAG 2.1 | Brand digital properties must meet accessibility standards; 4.5:1 contrast minimum for normal text |

## Process

### Phase 1: Brand Audit & Baseline (Weeks 1–6)
1. Inventory all brand-bearing assets across BUs, channels, partners, geographies
2. Map current architecture — every parent-sub-brand relationship, orphaned legacy brands
3. Stakeholder perception research: customer tracker, employee alignment survey, partner audit
4. Benchmark equity metrics against competitors using Keller CBBE dimensions
5. Assess governance maturity: approval workflows, DAM adoption, guideline freshness, violation frequency
6. Document legal IP portfolio: registered trademarks, pending applications, domains, social handles

### Phase 2: Architecture & Strategy Definition (Weeks 7–14)
7. Define/revalidate architecture model based on audit and growth strategy (organic vs. acquisition)
8. Establish brand roles: strategic growth driver, cash cow, flanker, or sunset candidate
9. Develop brand positioning per active brand: purpose, value proposition, personality, tone, audience
10. Create Brand Key — single-page artifact anchoring all creative and communications decisions
11. Define measurement scorecard: leading (search volume, SOV, awareness) and lagging (NPS, preference, price premium, CLV) indicators

### Phase 3: Guidelines & Governance Build (Weeks 15–24)
12. Develop guidelines: logo, color system (Pantone/CMYK/RGB/HEX + WCAG contrast ratios), typography, photography, iconography, data viz, motion, voice/tone matrices by channel
13. Build/configure DAM (Frontify, Bynder, Brandfolder) with metadata taxonomy, rights management, expiration alerts, usage analytics
14. Design tiered approval workflows: Tier 1 = brand identity changes (CMO); Tier 2 = campaign creative (brand team); Tier 3 = templated assets (self-serve)
15. Create training curriculum: onboarding module, quarterly refreshers, partner/agency certification
16. Establish automated compliance monitoring for logo misuse, color deviation, messaging drift

### Phase 4: Activation & Enforcement (Ongoing)
17. Launch brand internally before externally — employee engagement predicts external strength
18. Deploy across priority touchpoints: digital → sales enablement → physical environments → partner materials
19. Quarterly brand compliance audits with scored reports per BU
20. Semi-annual equity tracking studies (continuous tracking for brands spending >$50M annually)
21. Crisis response playbook updated quarterly; tabletop exercises minimum twice per year

### Phase 5: Crisis Response (Event-Triggered)
22. Detection: social listening flags anomaly (volume spike, sentiment drop, keyword triggers)
23. Assessment: severity tier evaluated within 60 minutes
24. Activation: crisis team convened (brand lead, legal, comms, BU head, C-suite if Tier 1)
25. Response: pre-approved holding statement within 2 hours; full response within 6 hours
26. Monitoring: adjust messaging every 4–6 hours based on response effectiveness
27. Recovery: brand health assessment at 30/60/90 days; update playbook with lessons learned

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Brand Architecture | Hierarchical structure defining parent-sub-brand relationships; a strategic framework, not a visual system |
| Branded House | Single master brand spans all products (e.g., Google, FedEx) |
| House of Brands | Parent invisible to consumers; each brand independent (e.g., P&G) |
| Brand Equity | Commercial value from consumer perception beyond functional product value; manifests as price premium, preference, loyalty |
| CBBE | Keller's Customer-Based Brand Equity pyramid: identity → meaning → response → relationships |
| Role of Brand Index (RBI) | Interbrand metric: % of purchase decision attributable solely to brand |
| Brand Dilution | Gradual equity erosion from overextension, inconsistent execution, or loss of distinctiveness |
| Brand Governance | System of policies, workflows, tools ensuring brand consistency across touchpoints |
| DAM | Digital Asset Management platform — single source of truth for brand assets (Frontify, Bynder, Brandfolder, AEM) |
| Brand Tracker | Research instrument measuring awareness/consideration/preference/usage/advocacy at regular intervals |
| Share of Voice (SOV) | Brand's proportion of total category advertising; SOV > SOM = market share gain predictor |
| NPS | Net Promoter Score = % Promoters (9–10) − % Detractors (0–6); lagging loyalty indicator |
| Brand Key | One-page strategic document: audience, environment, insight, benefits, values, personality, RTB, discriminator |
| Holding Statement | Pre-approved statement deployed within first 2 hours of crisis; acknowledges situation without admitting fault |
| Brand Salience | Degree brand comes to mind in a buying situation; Byron Sharp's primary market share driver |
| Price Premium | % above category average a brand sustainably commands; direct financial expression of equity |
| Brand Stretch | Distance brand can extend into adjacent categories while maintaining credibility |
| War Room Protocol | Highest-severity crisis activation: CEO, CLO, CMO, CISO in continuous coordination |
| Endorsement Architecture | Sub-brands with visible parent endorsement (e.g., Courtyard by Marriott) |
| Brand Contribution | BrandZ metric isolating consumer demand generated by brand alone |
| Adaptive Governance | AI-powered compliance checks embedded at point of content creation; replaces static approval workflows |
| Brand Resonance | Highest CBBE level: behavioral loyalty, attitudinal attachment, community, active engagement |
| Masterbrand | Primary highest-level brand from which portfolio brands derive or separate equity |

## Checklist

- [ ] Brand architecture model explicitly defined with rationale and rules for placing new products/acquisitions
- [ ] Every active brand has current Brand Key updated within last 12 months
- [ ] Guidelines cover both visual AND verbal identity with channel-specific tone modulation
- [ ] Color specs include WCAG 2.1 AA compliant combinations (4.5:1 minimum contrast for normal text)
- [ ] DAM platform operational with >60% monthly active user rate among eligible creators/distributors
- [ ] Brand tracker fielded minimum quarterly with results dashboarded alongside business KPIs
- [ ] Crisis playbook exists with severity tiers, spokespeople, pre-approved holding statements; rehearsed via tabletop within last 6 months
- [ ] All trademarks have current registration status; calendar reminders set for USPTO Section 8 (years 5–6) and Section 8/9 (years 9–10)
- [ ] FTC endorsement compliance protocols documented and communicated to influencer/advocacy/testimonial teams
- [ ] Brand equity measurement integrates ≥2 frameworks
- [ ] Internal brand engagement score measured annually
- [ ] Post-acquisition brand integration timeline and architecture decision deadline (<90 days recommended) defined in M&A playbook
- [ ] Competitive brand benchmarking conducted semi-annually: SOV, perception gaps, positioning differentiation
- [ ] Brand governance includes documented escalation path from coaching to corrective action with organizational authority
