## Role
Systematically collect, synthesize, and activate competitive intelligence to improve win rates, sharpen positioning, and accelerate deal cycles.

## CI Maturity Model

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 — Ad Hoc | Reps Google competitors; no repository; battlecards in scattered Docs; loss reasons = CRM picklists |
| 2 — Reactive | PMM produces quarterly decks; battlecards updated infrequently; one-way intelligence flow; no formal W/L program |
| 3 — Structured | Dedicated CI owner; battlecards updated monthly with automated tools; third-party W/L interviews on subset; competitive win rate tracked |
| 4 — Integrated | CI embedded in deal workflows via CRM/Slack; dynamic battlecards served contextually from Gong/Chorus; continuous W/L program; influenced revenue tracked |
| 5 — Predictive | AI-powered W/L models; CI informs roadmap/pricing/M&A; real-time alerts trigger automated playbook recommendations; compounding intelligence hub |

## Competitive Deal Decision Tree

| Axis | Evaluation |
|------|-----------|
| Threat Assessment | Tier 1 = top 3 direct (70%+ of encounters); Tier 2 = emerging/adjacent; Tier 3 = niche/long-tail; tier determines response intensity |
| Deal Stage | Early = quick dismiss; Mid = landmine-setting + differentiation; Late = full battlecard + exec engagement |
| Buyer Perception Gap | W/L data reveals systematic buyer misconceptions to address |
| Competitive Advantage | Sustainable (platform architecture, data moats, ecosystem lock-in) vs. perishable (feature parity, current pricing) |

## Program Scorecard

| Dimension | Weight | Scoring (1–5) |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| Collection breadth | 10% | 1=manual only; 5=automated multi-source |
| Signal-to-noise ratio | 15% | 1=overwhelming alerts; 5=curated actionable insights |
| Battlecard quality & freshness | 20% | 1=stale/generic; 5=updated monthly with field validation |
| Sales adoption rate | 20% | 1=<10% usage; 5=>70% with CRM/Slack integration |
| Win/loss program maturity | 15% | 1=CRM picklists only; 5=third-party buyer interviews with trend analysis |
| Competitive win rate improvement | 15% | 1=no measurable change; 5=>20% lift in competitive deals |
| Time-to-intelligence | 5% | 1=>1 week; 5=<30 minutes for critical alerts |

## Battlecard Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Adoption rate | >40% of reps accessing in competitive deals; top quartile >70% |
| Freshness | Updated monthly; monthly-updated teams see up to 59% win rate lifts (Crayon) |
| Seller confidence score | ≥7/10 per competitor; <6 triggers targeted enablement |
| Time-to-intelligence | <30 minutes for critical alerts (documented: 27-min delivery improved win rate 32%→67%) |

## Legal/Ethics Framework

| Law | Applicability |
|-----|--------------|
| Economic Espionage Act (18 U.S.C. §§1831–1839) | Criminalizes trade secret misappropriation; fines up to $5M; imprisonment up to 10 years |
| Defend Trade Secrets Act (DTSA, 2016) | Federal civil cause of action for trade secret misappropriation; relevant when hiring competitor employees |
| Uniform Trade Secrets Act (UTSA) | Adopted by 48 states; defines misappropriation at state level |
| EU Trade Secrets Directive 2016/943 | Harmonizes trade secret protection across EU |
| GDPR | Collecting personal data on competitor employees requires lawful basis |
| FTC Act §5 | CI-derived competitor claims in sales materials must be truthful and substantiated |
| SCIP Code of Ethics | Always disclose identity; respect confidentiality; avoid conflicts; comply with all laws |

**Ethics test:** Apply both "newspaper test" (embarrassing if reported?) AND "legal test" (attorney would flag?). If either = yes → do not proceed. Legitimate sources: public filings, patents, analyst reports, customer reviews, job postings, press releases, conference presentations, disclosed buyer interviews.

## Process

### Phase 1: Intelligence Collection (Ongoing)
1. Configure automated monitoring: competitor websites (pricing, careers, changelog, IR), G2/Capterra/TrustRadius, SEC filings, patent databases, social channels
2. Establish field intelligence channels — Chrome extensions or email addresses for rep submissions (Klue/Crayon support natively)
3. Instrument conversation intelligence — configure Gong/Chorus/Clari to flag competitor mentions by name, deal stage, context
4. Monitor "dark data" signals: job postings (hiring Kubernetes engineers = cloud-native pivot), executive departures, office lease changes
5. Track non-traditional sources: regulatory filings, partner announcements, conference speaking slots, advisory board disclosures; 42% of competitive threats emerge from non-traditional sources

### Phase 2: Intelligence Synthesis
6. Score and prioritize signals using impact/confidence matrix; high-impact/high-confidence → immediate alerts; low-confidence → watch lists
7. Build tiered battlecards: Universal (1-page overview: snapshot, differentiators, quick dismiss, top 3 landmines); Role-Based (BDR=prospecting, AE=mid-deal, SE=technical); Dynamic/Matrixed (segment/persona-specific)
8. Craft 3 critical battlecard elements per competitor:
   - **Quick Dismiss**: Acknowledge competitor → concede minor strength → pivot to weakness contrasted with your value; used early to prevent full competitive evaluation
   - **Landmine Questions**: Open-ended questions designed for buyer to ask competitor, targeting known deficiencies they cannot answer; always phrased as questions, never statements; must be validated against current competitor capabilities within 60 days
   - **Objection Reframes**: Responses to top 3–5 competitor claims about you backed by third-party data, customer quotes, or analyst validation

### Phase 3: Win/Loss Analysis Program
9. Prioritize late-stage deals where buyers saw full demos and pricing; research cohorts of 10+ similar deals; target 50/50 wins-to-losses ratio
10. Use third-party interviewers or AI-assisted async interviews (e.g., User Intuition); reps wrong about loss reasons >60% of the time (Clozd)
11. Analyze 4 dimensions: product/feature gaps, sales experience quality, pricing/value perception, brand/trust factors; track trends quarterly
12. Distribute: executive summaries (QBRs), competitive trend reports (product), tactical W/L presentations with objection scripts (frontline)

### Phase 4: Competitive Response Strategy
13. Categorize competitor moves: Ignore (noise, no market impact) / Monitor (emerging, pre-revenue) / Counter (requires battlecard update) / Pre-empt (move first based on intelligence)
14. Deploy proactive competitive plays: engage stakeholders before competitors establish footholds; activate references countering competitor narratives
15. Measure: competitive win rate by competitor, battlecard adoption, influenced revenue, time-to-intelligence

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Battlecard | Internal 1–2 page reference with talk tracks, objection handlers, differentiation statements, and landmine questions for specific competitors |
| Quick Dismiss | Verbal technique acknowledging competitor, conceding minor strength, pivoting to weakness; prevents full competitive evaluation |
| Landmine Question | Open-ended question for buyer to ask competitor exposing known deficiency; always a question, never a statement |
| Win/Loss Analysis | Post-decision buyer research program on both wins and losses to understand actual purchase drivers |
| Competitive Win Rate | % of deals won when specific competitor is present; primary CI program KPI |
| Influenced Revenue | Revenue from closed deals where seller accessed CI deliverables within defined time window |
| Tier 1/2/3 Competitors | 1=top 3 in 70%+ of competitive deals; 2=emerging/adjacent; 3=niche/infrequent |
| Competitive Encounter Rate | % of pipeline deals with ≥1 identified competitor; enterprise B2B average: 60–80% |
| Signal-to-Noise Ratio | Proportion of CI alerts resulting in actionable intelligence vs. irrelevant notifications |
| Objection Reframe | Structured response redirecting from competitor's chosen ground to terrain where your solution excels |
| Field Intelligence | Real-time observations from frontline sellers, SEs, and CSMs during deals and accounts |
| Dark Data | Unstructured organizational data (emails, calls, Slack) containing competitive signals; ~90% of enterprise data |
| M&CI | Market and Competitive Intelligence; expanded paradigm monitoring ecosystem (regulatory, technology, partner, substitute) not just direct competitors |
| Competitive Enablement | Function making CI accessible and actionable for sellers at point of need |
| FUD | Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt; effective only when backed by verifiable facts; unsubstantiated FUD destroys credibility |
| Intelligence Hub | Queryable knowledge base accumulating CI over time; compounding, searchable — not a static report repository |
| Counterintelligence | Protecting your own organization's sensitive information from competitor CI efforts |

## Checklist

- [ ] Competitive encounter data validated: CRM tracks competitor presence with >80% accuracy cross-referenced against conversation intelligence
- [ ] Every Tier 1 competitor battlecard updated within last 30 days with documented change log and named owner
- [ ] W/L sample size adequate: minimum 10 buyer interviews per cohort (per competitor, per segment) before drawing conclusions
- [ ] Buyer interviews conducted by party independent of sales relationship — third-party firm, separate research team, or AI interviewer
- [ ] Battlecard access rates measured at individual rep level; correlated to competitive win rates
- [ ] Every quick dismiss and landmine validated against current competitor capabilities within last 60 days; outdated ones flagged and removed
- [ ] All CI collection methods reviewed against SCIP Code of Ethics and applicable trade secret law; no intelligence from confidential sources or misrepresentation
- [ ] CI findings reach product management (feature gaps), marketing (messaging), and customer success (retention risk) — not just sales
- [ ] Documented response plans for top 3 competitive scenarios reviewed quarterly
- [ ] Measurable link between CI deliverable access and deal outcomes documented and accepted by sales leadership and finance
- [ ] ≥20% of customer-facing staff contribute competitive observations monthly
- [ ] VP-level+ executive sponsor reviews CI reporting monthly and has acted on findings within last quarter
- [ ] Quarterly survey shows avg rep confidence selling against each Tier 1 competitor ≥7/10; scores <6 trigger enablement intervention
- [ ] Resource allocation across competitor tiers reflects actual encounter frequency and loss rate data — not assumptions
