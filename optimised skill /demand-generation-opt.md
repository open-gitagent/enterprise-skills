## Role
Design and operate integrated demand generation systems across inbound/outbound programs, lead nurturing, and MQL/SQL frameworks to build pipeline and drive revenue across the full buyer journey.

## Foundational Decision Framework

| Decision | Logic |
|----------|-------|
| ICP-First vs. Volume-First | ICP-first: 80/20 analysis on existing customers → target best-fit accounts specifically; consistently outperforms on cost-per-opportunity, win rate, deal size |
| Demand Creation vs. Capture Ratio | Early-stage / low brand awareness: 60–70% creation; established / competitive market: 50–60% capture; creation = influence the 95% not yet buying |
| Lead-Based vs. ABM | Deals >$50K ACV with 6+ stakeholder buying committees → ABM; <$15K ACV with individual decision-makers → lead-based |
| Primary Measurement Lens | Pipeline Per Dollar (not CPL) + Pipeline Velocity = (Qualified Opps × Avg Deal Value × Win Rate) ÷ Avg Sales Cycle Length |

## Funnel Health Scorecard

| Metric | Below Average | Average | Top Performer |
|--------|--------------|---------|--------------|
| Lead → MQL conversion | <20% | 34–41% | >50% |
| MQL → SQL conversion | <10% | 13–22% | 25–40% |
| SQL → Opportunity | <25% | 36–42% | >50% |
| Opportunity → Closed-Won | <15% | 20–30% | >35% |
| Avg sales cycle length | >12 months | 8–10 months | <6 months |
| Pipeline velocity (daily) | <$5K | $10–25K | >$50K |
| Marketing-sourced pipeline % | <20% | 30–40% | >50% |
| CAC payback period | >18 months | 12–15 months | <10 months |

## Program Scoring Model (score each 1–5)

- ICP alignment: program specifically reaches defined Tier 1/2 accounts and personas
- Stage appropriateness: content/offer matches buying stage of target audience
- Multi-threading potential: program engages multiple buying committee members
- Measurability: pipeline attribution can be tracked to this program
- Scalability: volume can increase without proportional cost increase
- Sales alignment: sales team values and follows up on outputs
- Velocity impact: program accelerates existing pipeline, not just creates new

## Regulatory Requirements

| Requirement | Key Obligation |
|-------------|---------------|
| CAN-SPAM Act (15 U.S.C. §7701–7713) | Applies to B2B commercial email; accurate headers; non-deceptive subjects; identify as advertisement; valid physical address; opt-out within 10 business days; penalties up to $51,744 per violation |
| GDPR Article 6(1)(f) — Legitimate Interest | B2B cold email to EU data subjects may be lawful under LI basis; requires documented Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA); EDPB Guidelines 1/2024 provides updated interpretation; B2C requires explicit opt-in |
| CASL (S.C. 2010, c.23) | Express or implied consent before commercial electronic messages; implied consent: 2 years after business relationship, 6 months after inquiry; penalties up to CAD $10M per violation |
| CCPA/CPRA (Cal. Civ. Code §1798.100–199) | Disclosure of data collection; right to opt out of sale/sharing; impacts third-party data usage in targeting |
| TCPA (47 U.S.C. §227) | Automated/prerecorded calls/texts to cell phones require prior express consent; B2B landlines generally exempt; penalties $500–$1,500 per violation |
| EU ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC) | Informed consent required for non-essential cookies; impacts retargeting pixels and attribution measurement |
| Third-party intent data vendors | Ensure compliant consent chains; purchasing data from providers without proper consent = GDPR exposure |

## Process

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
1. Run 80/20 analysis on existing customers — identify top 20% by revenue; document firmographic attributes, behavioral patterns, buying committee composition
2. Conduct 8–12 customer interviews: what triggered search, who was involved, what sources they trusted, what almost disqualified you, what tipped the decision
3. Define ICP and tiered account lists: Tier 1 (exact ICP, 100–500 accounts for ABM); Tier 2 (strong fit, 500–2,000 for targeted programs); Tier 3 (addressable market for broad programs)
4. Map buying committee per ICP: title variations, responsibilities, pain points, info consumption habits, role in decision (champion/economic buyer/technical evaluator/end user/blocker)
5. Build Dream 100 influence map: 100 media properties, communities, podcasts, events, influencers, publications where ICP accounts consume information

### Phase 2: Infrastructure (Weeks 3–6)
6. Define lead lifecycle stages (co-owned by marketing and sales): Anonymous Visitor → Known Contact → Engaged Lead → MQL → SAL → SQL → Opportunity → Closed-Won/Lost; document entry criteria, exit criteria, SLAs per stage
7. Build lead scoring model combining: Fit score (firmographic/technographic ICP match) + Intent score (Bombora/G2/6sense third-party signals) + Engagement score (owned property behavior); weight Fit most heavily
8. Configure nurture architecture: minimum 3 tracks (by buying stage, persona/role, pain point cluster), 6–8 touches each, 4–8 weeks spacing, multi-format (text email, video, case study, third-party validation)
9. Establish measurement: UTM taxonomy, CRM campaign tracking, W-shaped multi-touch attribution (30% first touch / 30% lead creation / 30% opportunity creation / 10% distributed), dashboard cadence

### Phase 3: Inbound Launch (Weeks 5–10)
10. Deploy content engine by buying stage: Awareness = original research, thought leadership, podcast; Consideration = comparison guides, webinars, solution content; Decision = case studies, ROI calculators, implementation guides; map each to persona and stage
11. Launch SEO/organic strategy: bottom-of-funnel keywords (comparisons, pricing, alternatives) for immediate pipeline; topical authority clusters for mid-term traffic
12. Activate organic social: prioritize LinkedIn; develop employee advocacy program (exec + SME posting cadence); distribute content natively, not just links
13. Build conversion infrastructure: high-intent pages (demo, contact, pricing) convert frictionlessly; low-intent pages offer ungated value with optional progressive profiling

### Phase 4: Outbound Launch (Weeks 6–12)
14. Deploy multi-channel outbound sequences (email + LinkedIn + phone) for Tier 1/2; personalize first touch with account-specific insight; 8–12 touches over 3–4 weeks
15. Launch paid media: LinkedIn Ads for title/company/industry targeting; Google Ads for high-intent search; retargeting on both platforms for known engagers; content syndication for Tier 2/3
16. Execute event/webinar strategy: host quarterly owned webinars addressing top ICP pain points; sponsor 2–3 industry events per quarter with pre/post-event outbound sequences

### Phase 5: Optimization (Ongoing)
17. Weekly: new engaged accounts, content consumption velocity, email response rates, ICP website traffic
18. Bi-weekly: MQL-to-SQL conversion review; categorize rejected MQLs by reason code; adjust scoring model
19. Monthly: full-funnel conversion analysis; compare against benchmarks; identify single biggest drop-off point
20. Quarterly: program-level ROI — pipeline-per-dollar and CAC by channel; kill underperformers; double down on top 2–3

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Demand Creation | Programs generating new awareness among accounts not yet in buying cycle; builds brand preference for future decisions |
| Demand Capture | Programs targeting accounts showing active buying intent; converts existing demand into pipeline |
| MQL | Marketing Qualified Lead: meets fit criteria AND exhibited behavioral engagement above threshold; co-owned definition required |
| SAL | Sales Accepted Lead: SDR/BDR reviewed, contacted, and confirmed meets initial qualification criteria |
| SQL | Sales Qualified Lead: validated through direct conversation for confirmed need, authority, active evaluation |
| Pipeline Velocity | (Qualified Opps × Avg Deal Value × Win Rate) ÷ Avg Sales Cycle Days; daily dollar value flowing to close |
| Forrester B2B Revenue Waterfall | Industry-standard buying-group-centric progression framework; replaced lead-centric SiriusDecisions Demand Waterfall |
| ICP | Ideal Customer Profile: firmographic/technographic/behavioral definition built from retrospective best-customer analysis |
| Buying Committee | Group of stakeholders (avg 10–11 in enterprise B2B) collectively influencing purchase; buyers complete ~70% of journey before vendor contact |
| Lead Scoring | Quantitative model assigning points: fit score + intent score + engagement score |
| Multi-Touch Attribution | Distributes pipeline credit across touchpoints; W-shaped (30/30/30/10) recommended starting model |
| Content Syndication | Distributes gated content through third-party publisher networks; produces top-of-funnel leads requiring 12–16 week nurture |
| ABM | Account-Based Marketing; three tiers: 1:1 (strategic, <200 accts), 1:few (200–1,000), 1:many (1,000–5,000) |
| Intent Data | Third-party signals (Bombora, G2, 6sense) indicating accounts actively researching your category |
| SLA | Marketing-sales agreement: lead follow-up timeframes (e.g., MQL contacted within 4 hours), disposition requirements, feedback mechanisms |
| Progressive Profiling | Collects additional data fields across multiple form conversions; reduces initial friction while building contact richness |
| Dark Funnel | ~60–70% of buyer journey in channels invisible to attribution: peer conversations, Slack communities, dark social shares |
| MEDDPICC | Enterprise sales qualification: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition |
| Dream 100 | Strategic list of 100 media properties/influencers/communities/events where ICP accounts form opinions; guides channel selection |
| Warm Outbound | Outreach to contacts showing prior engagement signal; 3–5× higher response rates than cold outbound |
| Cost-Per-Opportunity (CPO) | Total program cost ÷ qualified opportunities generated; primary efficiency metric for program comparison |
| SQO | Sales Qualified Opportunity: passed full sales qualification; active pipeline with defined next step, confirmed stakeholder engagement, validated business case |

## Checklist

- [ ] ICP definition validated against closed-won data — criteria reflect patterns from top 20% of customers by revenue/LTV
- [ ] Lead scoring model back-tested against 6–12 months of historical closed-won/lost data; highest-scoring leads correlate with higher close rates
- [ ] MQL/SAL/SQL definitions documented and co-signed by marketing, SDR leadership, and sales leadership; stored in versioned shared document
- [ ] Nurture sequences segmented by ≥2 dimensions (buying stage AND persona/role)
- [ ] Multi-touch attribution active; UTM parameters enforced across all channels; attribution data reconciles with CRM pipeline within 5% variance
- [ ] Compliance verified: CAN-SPAM physical address/unsubscribe in all commercial emails; GDPR LIAs documented for EU outreach; CASL consent records maintained
- [ ] Sales follow-up SLA monitored: MQLs not contacted within SLA window trigger automated escalation
- [ ] Funnel conversion rates benchmarked; single biggest drop-off point identified; improvement hypothesis documented
- [ ] Every active demand generation program reports pipeline generated, pipeline influenced, and CPO — not just lead volume
- [ ] Rejected MQL feedback loop operational: sales dispositioning with reason codes reviewed monthly by marketing
- [ ] Every content asset tagged with target persona, buying stage, and primary CTA; content matrix gaps documented
- [ ] Intent data integrated and actioned (account prioritization, ad activation, outreach sequences) — not sitting unactioned
- [ ] Pipeline velocity tracked monthly as trend metric; decomposable into 4 component variables
- [ ] Budget reallocated quarterly based on actual CPO by channel — not historical allocation inertia
- [ ] Self-reported attribution collected (free-text "How did you hear about us?" on demo request forms) to capture dark funnel demand
