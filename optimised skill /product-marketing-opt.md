## Role
Translate product capabilities into market-facing value through positioning, messaging architecture, launch execution, competitive intelligence, and sales enablement for sales-led, product-led, or hybrid GTM motions.

## Launch Tier Classification

| Tier | Scope | Typical Assets | Cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (Scream) | New product/category, new market entry, company-redefining | Full press kit, AR/PR briefings, event keynote, analyst report, full campaign, exec blog, customer launch partner, dedicated landing page, sales training | 2-3/year max |
| Tier 2 (Shout) | Major feature, new integration, significant capability expansion | Blog, webinar, email campaign, updated battlecards, sales one-pager, social campaign | Quarterly |
| Tier 3 (Cheer) | Incremental improvement, parity feature, UX enhancement | Release notes, in-app notification, changelog, CSM talking points | Monthly |
| Tier 4 (Chirp) | Bug fix, minor update, backend improvement | Changelog entry only | Continuous |

## Decision Stack

| Layer | Decision | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Market Context | Who are buyers comparing against? | Dunford: identify realistic competitive alternatives including "do nothing" and "build in-house" |
| Audience Segmentation | Buyer (budget) vs. user vs. technical persona? | Pragmatic Institute: distinct messaging tracks per persona; conflating produces messaging that resonates with neither |
| Value Architecture | Capabilities → functional value → business outcomes → emotional resonance | Hierarchy: Category Narrative → Company Story → Product Positioning → Feature Messaging |
| Motion Selection | Sales-led/PLG/partner/hybrid? | PLG: in-app onboarding + activation + self-serve docs; SLG: battlecards + ROI calc + exec pitch deck; hybrid: both with shared positioning |

## Positioning Effectiveness Metrics

| Metric | Measurement | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive Win Rate | CRM closed-won ÷ (won + lost) against named competitors | >50% against each primary competitor |
| Message Comprehension | Buyer testing: % accurately paraphrasing core value proposition | >80% |
| Message Differentiation | Buyer testing: % identifying messaging as distinct from competitors | >70% |
| Sales Asset Adoption | Enablement platform analytics: % reps accessing assets in past 30 days | >75% |
| Content Influence on Pipeline | Attribution: deals consuming PMM content pre-opportunity creation | >30% of pipeline |
| Launch Tier Execution Score | % tier-required assets delivered on-time/on-quality | 100% Tier 1; >90% Tier 2 |
| Win/Loss Interview Completion | % closed deals (won+lost) with completed buyer interviews | >30% of all closed opportunities |
| NPS — Sales | Quarterly survey: "How useful is PMM content in your deals?" (1-10) | >8 |

## Process

**Phase 1 — Market Discovery (Weeks 1-4)**
1. Conduct win/loss analysis on 20-30 closed-lost and closed-won deals: structured 30-min buyer interviews (Clozd/Klue methodology; recorded + transcribed)
2. Build/refresh competitive landscape: 3-5 primary competitors, 2-3 adjacent/emerging threats, "do nothing" alternative
3. Validate buyer personas with sales: title, reporting structure, evaluation criteria, information sources, objection patterns
4. Document distinctive competencies (Pragmatic Institute): what can the organization uniquely deliver that the market values?
5. Rank market problems by urgency (how painful), pervasiveness (how many affected), willingness to pay

**Phase 2 — Positioning & Messaging (Weeks 4-8)**
6. Run positioning workshop using Dunford's methodology: Competitive Alternatives → Unique Attributes → Functional Value → Emotional Value → Target Segments → Market Category
7. Draft messaging hierarchy: one strategic narrative (90 sec), 3 value pillars, persona-specific proof points, objection-handling scripts
8. Validate messaging with buyer testing (Wynter/UserTesting; 10-15 target-persona participants); measure comprehension, believability, differentiation
9. Create internal positioning document (1-2 pages): target market, competitive alternatives, differentiated value, proof points, market category claim

**Phase 3 — Content & Enablement Build (Weeks 8-14)**
10. Map buyer journey content matrix: Awareness (thought leadership/category) → Consideration (comparison guides/analyst/case studies) → Decision (ROI calculators/technical architecture/security-compliance sheets/pricing)
11. Build competitive battlecards per primary competitor: Positioning (theirs vs. ours), Strengths/Weaknesses, Landmines (questions exposing gaps), Trap-Setting Questions (discovery leading to your strengths), Objection Responses, Recent Intel
12. Develop sales enablement toolkit: pitch deck (12-15 slides), persona one-pager, demo talk track, discovery question guide, email templates by buyer stage
13. Build customer evidence library: case studies by industry/use-case/company-size, video testimonials, ROI metrics database

**Phase 4 — Launch Execution (Weeks 14-18)**
14. Assign launch tier; build tier-appropriate asset checklist
15. Conduct sales training: 45-min live session (positioning changes, competitive updates, new talk tracks, asset walkthrough) + recorded reference version
16. Execute launch sequence: internal enablement (Day -7) → AR/PR briefings (Day -3) → customer communications (Day 0) → public launch (Day 0) → demand gen (Day +1) → social amplification (Days 0-14)
17. Monitor launch metrics: first-week content engagement, sales asset adoption, pipeline influenced within 30 days

**Phase 5 — Post-Launch Optimization (Ongoing)**
18. Review win/loss data monthly: competitive win rate changes, primary loss reasons, messaging effectiveness scores
19. Refresh battlecards quarterly or upon significant competitive movement
20. Quarterly sales feedback sessions: what's working, what's missing, where are deals lost that shouldn't be

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Positioning | Strategic framework defining product perception relative to competitive alternatives for a specific buyer segment; governs all downstream messaging |
| Messaging Hierarchy | Structured document: strategic narrative → value pillars → persona proof points → feature-level claims |
| Battlecard | 1-2 page competitive reference for sales: competitor positioning/strengths/weaknesses/landmines/trap-setting questions/objection responses |
| GTM Motion | Strategic approach: sales-led/product-led/partner-led/community-led; determines enablement asset priorities |
| Launch Tier | Tier 1-4 classification determining marketing investment and cross-functional coordination for a product release |
| Win/Loss Analysis | Systematic post-deal buyer interviews; conducted outside the deal team to avoid bias; tracks competitive win rates, objection themes, process friction |
| Sales Enablement | Equipping revenue teams with content/training/tools; PMM owns messaging, competitive positioning, buyer-facing content |
| Buyer Persona | Research-backed archetype: title/role/goals/evaluation criteria/information sources/objection patterns; distinct from user personas |
| ICP | Ideal Customer Profile: firmographic/technographic definition of most likely buyer |
| PQL | Product Qualified Lead: user experiencing product value in trial/freemium exhibiting purchase-readiness behaviors |
| Competitive Frame of Reference | Set of alternatives buyer actually evaluates ("compared to what?"); anchors all positioning |
| Value Proposition Canvas | Strategyzer framework: customer Jobs/Pains/Gains mapped to Pain Relievers/Gain Creators/Products/Services |
| Distinctive Competencies | Pragmatic Institute: capabilities uniquely possessed creating differentiated customer value (not features) |
| Content Influence | Attribution: pipeline/revenue where PMM content consumed before opportunity creation |
| Trap-Setting Questions | Discovery questions leading buyer toward requirements where your product excels and competitors struggle |
| Landmine Questions | Questions planted with champion inside buying org to expose competitor weakness during evaluation |
| Category Creation | Defining/naming a new market category; requires AR investment + sustained content + 18-24 months |
| Analyst Relations (AR) | Engaging Gartner/Forrester/IDC to influence Magic Quadrant/Wave/MarketScape coverage that enterprise buyers rely on |
| Sales Play | Structured, repeatable motion for specific scenario with trigger events/discovery questions/messaging/proof/content sequence |
| Message Testing | Quantitative validation with target buyer panels (Wynter): comprehension/believability/relevance/differentiation |
| Attach Rate | % of deals/customers adopting specific product/module; measures cross-sell PMM effectiveness |
| Strategic Narrative | Company-level story arc (Andy Raskin): market shift → winners/losers → promised land → product as path |
| Champion Enablement | Arming internal buying-committee advocate with content/data/talking points to sell internally |
| POV Content | Thought leadership taking definitive stance on market trend; establishes credibility before product-level conversations |

## Checklist

- [ ] Positioning document current (≤6 months), validated with buyer testing, signed off by PMM/product/sales leadership
- [ ] Messaging hierarchy covers all 4 levels: strategic narrative, value pillars (≤3), persona-specific proof points, feature claims with evidence
- [ ] Competitive battlecards for each primary competitor updated ≤90 days: trap-setting questions, landmines, objection responses
- [ ] "Do Nothing"/status quo battlecard exists: addresses inertia/switching costs/why-change-now with quantified cost-of-inaction
- [ ] Launch tier system documented and aligned with product management and exec team before any launch planning begins
- [ ] Buyer personas validated against recent (≤6 months) sales data and customer interviews
- [ ] Sales enablement content mapped to Awareness/Consideration/Decision stages with gap analysis completed
- [ ] Win/loss analysis active: ≥30% of closed deals have buyer interviews conducted outside the deal team
- [ ] All marketing claims have documented substantiation per FTC Advertising Substantiation Policy
- [ ] Customer testimonials/case studies have proper disclosures per 16 CFR Part 255 (updated 2023); results represent typical experience or include disclaimers
- [ ] Sales asset adoption measured via Highspot/Seismic/Showpad; assets with <25% adoption flagged for retirement/replacement
- [ ] Content localized (not just translated) per major market: competitive landscape, regulatory context, cultural norms
- [ ] Competitive claims verified for factual accuracy; legal review of comparison pages per Lanham Act §43(a) (15 USC §1125(a))
- [ ] Post-launch measurement cadence: 7-day/30-day/90-day reviews of content engagement/pipeline influence/win rate/sales feedback
